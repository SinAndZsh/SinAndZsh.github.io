AI does not have to be built this way. Traditionally, the goal of AI was to solve problems in ways that simulatedsimulate /ˈsɪmjuleɪt/ v. 模拟 human mental processes. Today’s approach to AI doesn’t try to describe the rules of human thought; instead, it gives a computer millions of examples to imitateimitate /ˈɪmɪteɪt/ v. 模仿. 文章大意

本文出自《大西洋月刊》2026年7月科技版块，聚焦生成式 AI 的工程效率危机。作者指出，为维持大语言模型运行，科技公司正抢购全球约七成高端内存，导致硬盘、笔记本价格上涨，数据中心耗电量激增，甚至需要改装喷气发动机供电。

文章核心论点在于：生成式 AI 的“大”并非优势，而是工程灾难。与传统软件的对数级扩展不同，大语言模型呈二次方扩展，模型越大边际收益越低，却必须以更快增速扩张才能维持进步。行业虽明知其低效，却因万亿估值、营销狂热和“规模定律”迷信而继续蛮力投入。

作者还对比了传统 AI 研究注重模拟人类思维、消耗资源更少的研究路径，以及当下“用海量数据模仿”的路线，警示在摩尔定律放缓的背景下，这种低效 AI 被集成进操作系统与日常软件，将让普通消费者也付出代价。文章最后以硅谷工程师对 AI 近乎宗教般的信念作结，质疑其连“写出高效软件”这一基本任务都无暇顾及。

阅读技巧提示

  • 抓数字信号：外刊科技文常用具体数字支撑论点（70 percent、175 billion parameters、1 trillion 等）。阅读时把数字与对应结论关联，可快速定位细节题答案。
  • 识别对比结构：本文大量运用对比（传统软件 vs. 生成式 AI、对数扩展 vs. 二次方扩展、旧 AI 路径 vs. 新路径），把握对比关系即可把握作者态度。
  • 理解术语：scale、logarithmic、quadratically、economy of scale 等是本文关键词，遇到术语先看上下文解释，再回查词表。
  • 推断作者态度：作者观点常藏于结尾句。本文末句 “Not even the basic task of writing efficient software” 以反讽收束，明确表达对行业忽视工程效率的批评。

长难句解析

Large language models such as ChatGPT and Claude are so resource-hungry that tech companies may be purchasing 70 percent of the world’s supply of high-end computer memory, causing a shortage.
主干为 “Large language models ... are so resource-hungry that ...”。so...that... 引导结果状语从句，说明大语言模型资源消耗之大；现在分词短语 “causing a shortage” 作结果状语，补充造成内存短缺的结果。
With generative AI, the work of building efficient, scalable systems has not been done, and the problem is exacerbated by the ever-larger generative-AI models, which have grown from 175 billion parameters in 2020 to more than 1 trillion today, according to independent estimates.
本句为并列复合句。第一分句主干 “the work ... has not been done”；第二分句主干 “the problem is exacerbated by ... models”，其中 which 引导非限制性定语从句，修饰 models，说明模型规模从 1750 亿增长到 1 万亿参数；“according to independent estimates” 为插入语，表明数据来源。
There is an almost-religious conviction among many in Silicon Valley that something mindlike could arise from LLMs, which are ultimately just statistical language-generating software—this, despite the software’s inability to recall basic facts, its lack of common sense, and its complete dissimilarity to a biological brain.
主干为 “There is an almost-religious conviction ... that ...”。that 引导同位语从句，解释 conviction 的内容；which 引导非限制性定语从句，修饰 LLMs；破折号后的 “despite ...” 为让步状语，列举 LLM 的不足：无法回忆基本事实、缺乏常识、与生物大脑完全不同。

阅读理解练习

主旨大意题
1. What is the main argument of the passage?
A) Generative AI is transforming education positively.
B) Generative AI suffers from poor engineering scalability and is driving up hardware costs.
C) Large language models will soon replace human workers.
D) Moore’s Law guarantees that AI costs will keep falling.
正确答案：B
解析：文章开篇即指出 AI 公司为确保系统在线而让全社会承担更高成本，随后从内存短缺、数据中心扩张、电力需求、扩展性差、收益递减等多个角度论证：生成式 AI 的工程可扩展性极差，可能是“有史以来部署过的最差技术”。B 项准确概括主旨。
细节理解题
2. According to the passage, how do large language models scale compared with traditional software?
A) They scale logarithmically, becoming cheaper per user as they grow.
B) They scale quadratically, requiring more time and memory as input grows.
C) They follow economy of scale, becoming cheaper with larger production.
D) They scale linearly, keeping costs stable as user numbers increase.
正确答案：B
解析：文章明确指出 “LLMs scale quadratically”，即输入增加时，时间和资源消耗增长更快；而传统软件通常实现对数级扩展（logarithmic scaling）。因此答案为 B。
推理判断题
3. What can be inferred about the author’s view on the current AI industry?
A) The author believes bigger models will inevitably lead to artificial general intelligence.
B) The author thinks AI companies are investing wisely in efficient research.
C) The author suggests the industry prioritizes marketing and brute-force scaling over engineering efficiency.
D) The author argues that Moore’s Law will soon solve AI’s cost problems.
正确答案：C
解析：作者在文中批评行业迷信“规模定律”、采用蛮力方法、将 LLM 整合进各种产品，并引用研究人员的话指出“你必须学会更高效”。最后一句“Not even the basic task of writing efficient software”点明作者认为行业忽视工程效率。C 项符合推断。
