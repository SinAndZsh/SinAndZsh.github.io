And then there's reading. In The Atlantic, Horowitch cites an analysis of data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics' American Time Use Survey — often described as the gold standard measure of how the country spends its days — which shows that fewer Americans report reading for pleasure on a given day, but those who do are spending more time doing so. If these numbers represent a crisis, it doesn't appear to be a new one.

Of course, the Time Use survey is just one research project, with its own limitations; it's compiled out of data from single interviews in which people are asked to recount what they did the day before the call. But even in other data sets, the decline of reading isn't that obvious, either. You can slice that data in different ways if you're focused on the popularity of fiction, say, or poetry, and certain slices do show declines of, say, eight percentage points over 20 years.

But in 2022 the N.E.A. survey found that 55.6 percent of American adults had read or listened to a book in the previous year. In 2008 the number was 54.3 percent. In 2002 the figure was 56.6 percent, prompting the N.E.A. to publish a hand-wringing report called "Reading at Risk." One survey reports that roughly half of Americans are reading and another reports that roughly three-quarters are, suggesting something about the limitations of this kind of data and the lessons we try to extract from it. But it probably tells us something that neither one so clearly illustrates the intuitive story of significant decline — and neither do other measures. The point is not that everything is fine or that smartphones and AI pose no challenges to attention and literacy. They clearly do. 阅读理解练习

1. 主旨大意题：What is the main argument of the passage?
  1. Smartphones have completely destroyed Americans' reading habits and the publishing industry.
  2. The decline in reading is mainly caused by young people's addiction to digital screens and social media.
  3. The popular narrative of a widespread reading decline is oversimplified and largely contradicted by multiple data sources.
  4. American colleges and universities have failed to maintain rigorous reading requirements for students.
正确答案：C
答案解析：文章开篇虽然摆出"阅读正在消亡"的普遍观点，但从第三段起作者连续引用美国劳工统计局、国家艺术基金会、皮尤研究中心、盖洛普等多家机构的数据进行反驳，核心论点是"简单化的阅读消亡叙事与数据不符"。A项与文章主旨相反；B项是作者明确反驳的观点（数据显示老年人而非年轻人阅读下降最多）；D项仅为文中提及的一个细节，非主旨。
2. 细节理解题：According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics' American Time Use Survey, which group contributed most to the overall decline in American daily reading time?
  1. Americans aged 15 to 24, whose reading time fluctuated over the years.
  2. Americans aged 25 to 34, who showed a modest increase in recent years.
  3. Americans aged 65 and up, whose daily reading time fell by nearly half.
  4. College freshmen, who professors believe are less prepared for academic reading.
正确答案：C
答案解析：根据文章第七段，65岁以上美国人的日均阅读时间从2003年的58.5分钟降至去年的约32.4分钟，"a drop that represents the lion's share of overall reading declines"（这一降幅占了整体阅读下降的最大份额）。A、B项中的年轻人群体实际上阅读时间有所上升；D项是文章开头提到的现象，但与Time Use Survey的数据无关。
3. 推理判断题：What can be inferred from the passage about the author's attitude toward technology's impact on reading?
  1. He believes smartphones and AI have absolutely no negative effect on reading habits.
  2. He is fundamentally optimistic that reading culture will naturally thrive in the digital age.
  3. He argues that technology is merely one factor among many complex social changes and warns against oversimplification.
  4. He advocates for restricting the use of digital devices to preserve traditional reading practices.
正确答案：C
答案解析：作者在文章结尾明确承认"smartphones and AI pose no challenges to attention and literacy. They clearly do"（智能手机和人工智能确实对注意力和读写能力构成挑战），但同时强调"the popular narrative... obscures more than it reveals"（流行叙事遮蔽了更多真相），呼吁"looking beyond the easy morality tale of screens versus books"（超越屏幕与书籍对立的简单道德寓言）。这体现了他反对简单化、主张复杂分析的态度。A项与原文矛盾；B项"fundamentally optimistic"过度推断；D项文中未提及。
