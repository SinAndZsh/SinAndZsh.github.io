📋 文章大意

本文选自《经济学人》社论板块，探讨了印度面临的一个环境悖论：严重的空气污染虽然在短期内降低了地表温度，却每年导致数百万人死于污染相关疾病。文章指出，印度需要同时推进清洁能源和空调普及——前者解决污染问题，后者应对日益加剧的热浪。作者分析了灌溉扩张和空气污染这两个"降温因素"，揭示了清洁空气反而会加剧高温的两难困境，并提出了加速可再生能源投资、降低空调税率等政策建议。

The Lesser of Two Evils

Turn on the air-con — If India's awful air pollution is ever solved, the country will become even hotter.

At the end of March the India Meteorological Department predicted a warmer-than-usual April, the first month of India's hot season. The forecast was soon proved right: in the first week of April temperatures in big cities were 3°C above normal. By the second, Delhi was suffering an intense heatwave. Even night-time minimums hit their highest in years. Heat-related illness soared.

India, always hot, has been getting hotter. The past decade was the warmest on record. Yet it has not been warming as quickly as the rest of the planet. In the past four decades temperatures over Earth's land mass have risen by 0.3°C per decade, and by 0.23°C at comparable latitudes. The figure for India is a mere 0.09°C.

Two things are responsible for keeping India relatively cooler. One is the expansion of irrigated land, the area of which has doubled since the 1980s. Moisture in the air lowers temperatures, but comes at the cost of increasing humidity, sometimes to dangerous levels. In the baking Indo-Gangetic plain a combination of temperatures of just 37°C — a nice day for many — with 90% humidity can be fatal.

The second cooling agent is horrific air pollution. During the day, particulate matter intercepts the sun's rays, absorbing heat. It also makes clouds more reflective. The combined effects lead to a comparatively cooler surface. It follows that there is a tension: if India achieves cleaner air, an unintended effect would be higher daytime temperatures.

Some call for natural, energy-neutral methods to deal with rising heat, such as painting buildings white, using less concrete, and covering roofs in reflective tiles or second roofs. These low-cost solutions help somewhat, but they are no match for a north-Indian heatwave. More air-conditioning is necessary. Those who can afford it are already fuelling a boom: annual sales doubled between 2020 and 2024. Often these machines clean air as well as cool it.

One problem is that too few people yet have air-con: just one in ten households owns a unit, whereas two-thirds do in China and four-fifths in Malaysia. Air-con also creates even more pollution. During a heatwave last year, it accounted for a fifth of power demand, much of it met by dirty thermal-energy plants which provide three-quarters of generation. Air-conditioners throw heat into their immediate environment, making conditions worse for those without them. The hydrofluorocarbons they use as coolants are powerful greenhouse gases.

What to do? The only rational path is for India to push for cleaner energy and more air-con, simultaneously. The country is getting many things right. The state and central pollution-control boards are responsible for cleaning up the air. Renewables, including hydro, make up 224GW of installed generation capacity of 472GW and there are ambitious plans for them to provide half of the total capacity of 1,000GW by 2030. There are new efficiency standards for air-conditioners.

Yet the country needs to move faster. Pollution in the northern plains routinely exceeds the maximum readings of air quality sensors. That means millions die from pollution-linked disease every year. And if the skies become clearer, temperatures will rise faster.

Speeding up land acquisition for solar projects, investing in the grid and reforming the market for purchasing and distributing power would accelerate private-sector investment in renewables. And a faster roll-out of air conditioning could be encouraged by lowering the tax on machines from 28%, the highest available rate (Apple's iPhones are charged just 18%). The government should lead by example, air-conditioning offices where citizens meet public servants, not just those of politicians and elite bureaucrats.

Across Asia, the Middle East and the Southern United States, air-conditioning has helped make cities dynamic and more productive. There is a way for India to combine that goal with cleaner air. It should seize it.

🔍 长难句解析

"In the baking Indo-Gangetic plain a combination of temperatures of just 37°C — a nice day for many — with 90% humidity can be fatal."
结构分析：
• 地点状语：In the baking Indo-Gangetic plain（在炎热的印度-恒河平原上）
• 主语：a combination of temperatures of just 37°C with 90% humidity（仅37°C的温度与90%湿度的组合）
• 插入语：— a nice day for many —（对许多人来说是个好天气）
• 谓语：can be（可能是）
• 表语：fatal（致命的）

翻译：在炎热的印度-恒河平原上，仅37°C的温度——对许多人来说还算舒适——加上90%的湿度就可能致命。

考点提示：插入语是考研英语长难句的常见特征，阅读时应先跳过插入语，抓住主干 "a combination...can be fatal"。
"It follows that there is a tension: if India achieves cleaner air, an unintended effect would be higher daytime temperatures."
结构分析：
• 主句：It follows that there is a tension（由此可知，存在一种矛盾）
• 形式主语结构：It follows that...（由此得出...）
• 冒号后为解释说明
• 条件状语从句：if India achieves cleaner air（如果印度实现了更清洁的空气）
• 主句：an unintended effect would be higher daytime temperatures（一个意外的效果将是更高的白天温度）

翻译：由此可知，存在一种矛盾：如果印度实现了更清洁的空气，一个意外的效果将是白天温度更高。

考点提示："It follows that..." 是常见的逻辑推导句式，表示"由此得出..."，在考研阅读中常用于引出结论。
"Speeding up land acquisition for solar projects, investing in the grid and reforming the market for purchasing and distributing power would accelerate private-sector investment in renewables."
结构分析：
• 主语由三个并列的动名词短语构成：
  1) Speeding up land acquisition for solar projects（加速太阳能项目的土地获取）
  2) investing in the grid（投资电网）
  3) reforming the market for purchasing and distributing power（改革电力采购和分配市场）
• 谓语：would accelerate（将加速）
• 宾语：private-sector investment in renewables（私营部门对可再生能源的投资）

翻译：加速太阳能项目的土地获取、投资电网建设、改革电力采购和分配市场，将加速私营部门对可再生能源的投资。

考点提示：并列动名词短语作主语是考研长难句的常见结构，需识别并列关系，避免遗漏信息。
"During a heatwave last year, it accounted for a fifth of power demand, much of it met by dirty thermal-energy plants which provide three-quarters of generation."
结构分析：
• 时间状语：During a heatwave last year（在去年的热浪期间）
• 主句：it accounted for a fifth of power demand（它占电力需求的五分之一）
• 独立主格结构：much of it met by dirty thermal-energy plants（其中大部分由污染严重的火电厂满足）
• 定语从句：which provide three-quarters of generation（这些火电厂提供了四分之三的发电量）

翻译：在去年的热浪期间，空调占电力需求的五分之一，其中大部分由提供四分之三发电量的污染严重的火电厂来满足。

考点提示："much of it met by..." 是独立主格结构（名词+分词），在考研阅读中频繁出现，需注意其逻辑主语与主句主语不同。

✏️ 阅读理解练习（考研风格）

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main argument of this article?

✅ 正确答案：B
解析：文章核心论点在第六段明确表述："The only rational path is for India to push for cleaner energy and more air-con, simultaneously." 文章揭示了印度面临的环境悖论——清洁空气反而会加剧高温，因此需要同时推进清洁能源和空调普及。A项与文章主张相反；C项虽然提到污染有降温效果，但并非文章主旨；D项被文章明确否定（"they are no match for a north-Indian heatwave"）。

2. 【细节理解题】According to the article, why has India been warming more slowly than the rest of the planet?

✅ 正确答案：C
解析：第三段和第四段明确指出两个降温因素：一是灌溉土地的扩张增加了空气湿度从而降低温度，二是严重的空气污染中颗粒物拦截阳光并使云层更具反射性。A项虽然提到纬度，但文章用纬度数据来对比说明印度升温较慢，而非原因；B项是文章后半部分提到的解决方案，不是升温慢的原因；D项与文章"India, always hot"的描述矛盾。

3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred about India's current air-conditioning situation from the passage?

✅ 正确答案：C
解析：第八段提到"And a faster roll-out of air conditioning could be encouraged by lowering the tax on machines from 28%, the highest available rate"，暗示28%的高税率是空调推广的障碍之一。A项与"just one in ten households owns a unit"矛盾；B项与"Those who can afford it"暗示许多人负担不起相矛盾；D项文章提到空调用电主要由污染严重的火电厂满足，暗示电网清洁度不足，且需要"investing in the grid"。

💡 考研阅读技巧

  • 关注文章的"悖论"结构：本文开篇即提出一个反直觉的环境悖论——污染反而降温，清洁空气反而加剧高温。考研阅读常以悖论或反常识观点开篇，抓住这一核心矛盾就能理解全文逻辑。
  • 数据对比是关键信息：文章多处使用数据对比（0.09°C vs 0.3°C、1/10 vs 2/3 vs 4/5、28% vs 18%），这些对比往往对应考点，需重点关注数字之间的比较关系。
  • 识别作者态度："The only rational path is..."、"The government should lead by example"等表达清楚表明作者立场。考研阅读中，社论类文章的作者态度通常在转折后明确出现。
  • 插入语和独立主格结构："— a nice day for many —"和"much of it met by dirty thermal-energy plants"分别是插入语和独立主格结构，是考研长难句的常见考点，阅读时可先跳过再回看。
  • 词汇积累重点：本文中 particulate、intercept、reflective、unintended、simultaneously、ambitious、routinely、accelerate 等是考研高频词汇，需重点记忆。
  • 逻辑连接词："It follows that..."（由此得出）、"Yet..."（然而）、"The only rational path is..."（唯一合理的路径是）等逻辑连接词标记了文章的论证脉络，有助于快速把握文章结构。
