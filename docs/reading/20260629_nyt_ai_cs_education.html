How Do You Teach Computer Science in the Age of AI?

Published: June 29, 2026 | Category: Technology & Education | Length: ~1,150 words
文章大意：本文探讨了在生成式人工智能迅速普及的背景下，美国高校如何重新思考计算机科学教育。随着AI工具能够自动编写代码，传统的编程教学面临挑战，教育者正将重心从语法记忆转向计算思维、AI素养和批判性判断能力的培养。文章采访了多位教授和学生，呈现出这场教育变革中的困惑、探索与希望。

Professors who had spent decades teaching students to write code line by line suddenly confronted tools that could produce entire programs in seconds. The question was no longer academic; it was existential. If machines could program, what exactly should humans be learning?

At Stanford University, Professor Mehran Sahami has watched this transformation/ˌtrænsfəˈmeɪʃn/ n. 转变；变革 unfold from the front lines. "We are not training people to be syntax/ˈsɪntæks/ n. 语法；句法 compilers," he said in a recent interview. "We are training them to be problem solvers." Some veteran/ˈvetərən/ n. 老手；经验丰富的人 educators worry that relying too heavily on AI tools will erode/ɪˈrəʊd/ v. 侵蚀；削弱 the foundational skills that have long defined the discipline. This concern has ignited/ɪɡˈnaɪt/ v. 点燃；引发 a lively debate about the proper role of AI in the classroom — a debate that remains far from settled.

Coding challenges still exist in interviews, but they are increasingly supplemented by questions that test a candidate's judgment about when to use AI and when to rely on human expertise.

Underlying all of these discussions is a deeper question about the purpose of education itself. One emerging consensus is that AI literacy — an understanding of how these systems work, where they fail, and how they shape society — should become as integral/ˈɪntɪɡrəl/ adj. 不可或缺的；构成整体所必需的 to the curriculum as data structures or software engineering. What is clear, however, is that the age of AI has forced educators to articulate/ɑːˈtɪkjuleɪt/ v. 明确表达；清楚说明 something they had long taken for granted: the precise value of human thought in a world of increasingly capable machines. And that, perhaps, is a lesson worth learning no matter what the future holds.
