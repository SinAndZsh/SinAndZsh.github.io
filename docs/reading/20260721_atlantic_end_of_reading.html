The End of Reading Is Here

文章大意

本文出自《大西洋月刊》2026年7月封面故事，作者Rose Horowitch深入探讨了美国社会正经历的"阅读终结"现象。文章指出，美国人正从曾经的"自豪的识字社会"滑向"后识字时代"（postliterate era）：2022年不到一半成年人读过任何书籍，高中生阅读理解能力降至三十年来最低，而社交媒体短视频和生成式AI正在加速这一趋势。作者通过大量数据、教育专家访谈和政治观察，警示这种阅读能力衰退不仅威胁个人认知发展，更可能动摇民主社会的根基。

Americans, once members of a proudly literate有读写能力的；受过良好教育的 society, read much less than they used to. According to the National Endowment for the Arts, which conducts the most comprehensive survey of the nation's reading habits, fewer than half of all adults reported having read a book of any kind in 2022. Only 38 percent read a novel or short story. A study analyzing 236,000 responses to the American Time Use Survey found that the proportion of Americans who read for pleasure on any given day fell from 28 percent in 2004 to 16 percent in 2023. Gambling has become a more common leisure activity than reading a book: Last year, 57 percent of Americans placed a bet.

In 2024, in a national test, just 35 percent of high-school seniors were "proficient熟练的；精通的" at skills such as analyzing complex fictional themes and evaluating the effectiveness of an author's argument. About the same number scored below "basic," meaning that they may struggle to draw conclusions from concepts explicitly included in a text, or to use context clues to determine the meaning of an unknown word. Adult-literacy scores have also dropped: Nearly 30 percent of American adults cannot paraphrase释义；改述 or make inferences from a multipage text. In 2017, that number was less than 20 percent.

Maryanne Wolf, a cognitive neuroscientist神经科学家 at UCLA, argues that people are losing the ability to think deeply about writing. That doesn't mean they are forgetting how to decode individual words. Rather, they are losing the higher-order abilities of comprehension and synthesis. America, in other words, isn't illiterate. It's postliterate后识字的；后文字时代的.

A 2025 survey found that most middle- and high-school English teachers assigned zero to four books a year. Successive waves of education reforms have led districts to favor short passages over full books, the better to mimic multiple-choice reading-comprehension exams. Many of the most popular school curricula课程（复数） now rely on excerpts. Annemarie Cortez, the principal at an elementary school in Corona, California, told me that many administrators are instructing teachers not to assign full books; they're supposed to be running discrete reading drills with short excerpts.

In the recent past, people were at least reading something online, but that's changing fast. Social media, once mainly text-based, has been overrun with short-form videos. TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels dominate the attention economy注意力经济, especially among young people. According to a recent data analysis by Jean Twenge, a psychology professor who studies generational change, by eighth grade, the average kid spends four and a half hours a day on social media. For much of that time, it appears, they are watching videos, often at 2x speed. Even text messages have taken on characteristics of the spoken word. People use all caps to indicate heightened emotion and avoid the formality of proper punctuation, which now seems stilted不自然的；呆板的, even stern.

Reading has come to seem extraneous多余的；无关紧要的 even to some of the best-educated members of society. Margaret Rennix, Harvard's assistant director for humanities and social-sciences support, told me she'd spoken with a student who was struggling to read a book written in Old English. The culprit: Anthony Burgess's 1962 novel A Clockwork Orange. (The student used ChatGPT to "translate" the book into easier language.) Not long ago, a Harvard sociology professor, troubled by course evaluations in which students said they resented怨恨；不满 the amount of dense reading they were assigned, asked Rennix to speak to his class in defense of reading. She had to explain — to students at America's most elite university, taking a course in a discipline rooted in written observation, argumentation, and analysis — that excerpts and summaries cannot capture the depth and sophistication of a complete primary text.

If Trump is the first postliterate president, he won't be the last. The political strategist David Plouffe, an architect of Barack Obama's presidential campaigns, recently argued that candidates should focus each day on content creation. He advised shrinking every idea into something short enough for screen-addled voters to concentrate on. "If it can't be communicated in an Instagram post or 10-second TikTok, go back to the drawing board," Plouffe wrote in a New York Times op-ed. That may very well be good advice on how to campaign for office in the postliterate era. As a way to practice informed self-government, it portends预示；预兆 disaster.

Americans are probably reading more words than ever before. What has changed is what they read, and how. People are bombarded轰炸；使应接不暇 with emails, text messages, X posts, Reddit threads, Instagram captions. This explosion of textual fragments has come at the expense of devoting sustained attention to longer written works that convey rich and complicated information. Kids watch more and more videos, and read less and less. While "video contains more information than text," watching videos is "a more passive form of engagement参与；投入 than reading" and generally "does not stimulate deeper thinking." Student reading and writing scores on standardized tests are at their lowest level in more than three decades.

When these students get to college, their professors find that they have to teach them how to comprehend理解；领悟 a text. Rennix told me that some students now view reading as an unnecessarily burdensome way of acquiring knowledge. "By asking them to read," she said, "professors are arbitrarily withholding information from students by forcing them to get it through this more difficult medium." A course's being difficult will, more and more, be understood as a professor being "difficult." When that is the prevailing普遍的；盛行的 interpretive framework, it is education that will be increasingly difficult.

Perhaps, 126 years from now, this essay will seem like the latest exercise in hand-wringing. But the data suggest otherwise. The decline in reading cuts across age groups, gender, and education levels. It is not a transient短暂的；暂时的 trend but a civilizational shift — one that threatens the very foundations of informed citizenship and critical thought.

阅读技巧提示

  • 数据定位法：本文包含大量统计数据（百分比、年份），阅读时可先快速浏览这些数字，帮助把握文章论证脉络。
  • 观点识别法：注意区分事实陈述（如"38 percent read a novel"）和作者观点（如"it portends disaster"），考研阅读常考此类区分。
  • 逻辑连接词：关注 "That doesn't mean... Rather..."、"In other words" 等逻辑转换，往往是出题点。
  • 例证功能：文中引用哈佛学生的例子，是为了说明什么观点？注意例子前后的总结句。

长难句解析

"A study analyzing 236,000 responses to the American Time Use Survey found that the proportion of Americans who read for pleasure on any given day fell from 28 percent in 2004 to 16 percent in 2023."

句子结构分析

主语：A study；谓语：found；宾语从句：that the proportion... fell...。其中 analyzing 236,000 responses 是现在分词短语作后置定语修饰 study；who read for pleasure 是定语从句修饰 Americans。句子的主干是 "A study found that the proportion fell from... to..."。

"She had to explain — to students at America's most elite university, taking a course in a discipline rooted in written observation, argumentation, and analysis — that excerpts and summaries cannot capture the depth and sophistication of a complete primary text."

句子结构分析

主语：She；谓语：had to explain；宾语从句：that excerpts and summaries cannot capture...。两个破折号之间是插入语，其中 "taking a course..." 是现在分词短语，"rooted in..." 是过去分词短语，层层修饰。注意多重后置定语的逐层拆解。

"By asking them to read, professors are arbitrarily withholding information from students by forcing them to get it through this more difficult medium."

句子结构分析

本句是引用部分，采用了 "By doing A, sb. is doing B by doing C" 的结构，即"通过做A，某人通过做C来实现B"。两个 by 短语分别表示方式和手段。arbitrarily（武断地）一词带有强烈感情色彩，暗示学生认为教授的做法不合理。

考研阅读理解练习

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of this article?
  • A. Americans should read more classic literature like Doctor Zhivago instead of popular novels.
  • B. American society is experiencing a profound decline in deep reading abilities, shifting toward a "postliterate" era.
  • B. American society is experiencing a profound decline in deep reading abilities, shifting toward a "postliterate" era. ✓
  • C. Social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram should be banned to protect young people's reading habits.
  • D. Harvard students are no longer capable of reading complex academic texts.

正确答案：B

本文开篇即指出美国人阅读量大幅下降，随后通过大量数据（2022年仅38%成年人读过小说、高中生阅读能力降至三十年最低等）论证社会正从"识字时代"滑向"后识字时代"。A选项过于片面，文中提及《日瓦戈医生》仅为对比；C选项的"禁止社交媒体"并非作者观点；D选项仅为文中一个例证，不能概括主旨。

2. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred from Margaret Rennix's experience at Harvard?
  • A. Harvard professors are assigning too much reading material to their students.
  • B. Students at elite universities prefer reading summaries to original texts.
  • B. Students at elite universities prefer reading summaries to original texts. ✓
  • C. A Clockwork Orange is too difficult for college students to understand.
  • D. ChatGPT has completely replaced traditional reading methods at universities.

正确答案：B

文中提到哈佛学生用ChatGPT"翻译"《发条橙》，以及有学生认为阅读是"unnecessarily burdensome way of acquiring knowledge"，甚至觉得教授"arbitrarily withholding information"。这些细节共同说明：即使是顶尖大学的学生，也倾向于选择更轻松的摘要或AI辅助，而非直接阅读原著。A选项与文意相反（教授被要求减少阅读量）；C选项过度推断；D选项"completely replaced"过于绝对。

3. 【词义猜测题】The word "portends" in Paragraph 7 is closest in meaning to ______.
  • A. prevents
  • B. delays
  • C. foretells ✓
  • D. causes

正确答案：C

"Portend"出现在句子 "As a way to practice informed self-government, it portends disaster" 中。前文提到Plouffe建议将政治理念压缩成短视频，作者认为这可能是好的竞选策略（"good advice on how to campaign"），但作为"informed self-government"（知情自治）的方式，则"portends disaster"。根据上下文逻辑，这里表示"预示、预兆"之意，与foretell（预言、预示）最接近。prevent（阻止）和delay（延迟）与文意相反；cause（导致）虽然接近，但portend更强调"预先显示征兆"而非直接"导致"。
