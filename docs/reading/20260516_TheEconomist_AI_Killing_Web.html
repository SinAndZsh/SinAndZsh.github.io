📋 文章大意

本文探讨了人工智能如何改变互联网的商业模式。随着ChatGPT等AI工具的兴起，用户越来越多地通过聊天机器人而非传统搜索引擎获取信息，这导致内容创作者面临流量锐减的困境。文章引用Cloudflare CEO Matthew Prince的数据：十年前Google每抓取两页内容会向网站发送一位访客，如今需要抓取六页才能换来一位访客；而OpenAI的抓取比例高达250:1，Anthropic更是达到6000:1。内容网站正在失去广告收入和订阅用户，许多出版商被迫与AI公司签订授权协议或采取法律行动。文章最后探讨了互联网可能面临的三种未来：内容创作者消亡、AI寡头垄断，或类似Netflix的内容竞争模式。

AI Is Killing the Web. Can Anything Save It?

Around the beginning of last year, Matthew Prince started receiving worried calls from the chief executives of large media companies. They told Mr Prince, whose firm, Cloudflare, provides security infrastructure to about a fifth of the web, that their businesses faced a grave new online threat.

"I said, 'What, is it the North Koreans?'," he recalls. "And they said, 'No. It's AI'."

Those executives had spotted the early signs of a trend that has since become clear: artificial intelligence is transforming the way that people navigate the web.

As users pose their queries to chatbots rather than conventional search engines, they are given answers, rather than links to follow. The result is that "content" publishers, from news providers and online forums to reference sites such as Wikipedia, are seeing alarming drops in their traffic.

As AI changes how people browse, it is altering the economic bargain at the heart of the internet. Human traffic has long been monetised using online advertising; now that traffic is drying up. Content producers are urgently trying to find new ways to make AI companies pay them for information. If they cannot, the open web may evolve into something very different.

Since the launch of ChatGPT in late 2022, people have embraced a new way to seek information online. OpenAI, maker of ChatGPT, says that around 800 million people use the chatbot. It is the most popular download on the iPhone app store. Apple said that conventional searches in its Safari web browser had fallen for the first time in April, as people posed their questions to AI instead.

As OpenAI and other upstarts have soared, Google, which has about 90 per cent of the conventional search market in America, has added AI features to its own search engine in a bid to keep up. Last year it began preceding some search results with AI-generated "overviews", which have since become ubiquitous. In May it launched "AI mode", a chatbot-like version of its search engine.

Yet as Google does the Googling, humans no longer visit the websites from which the information is gleaned. Similarweb, which measures traffic to more than 100m web domains, estimates that worldwide search traffic (by humans) fell by about 15 per cent in the year to June. Although some categories, such as hobbyists' sites, are doing fine, others have been hit hard.

Many of the most affected are just the kind that might have commonly answered search queries. Science and education sites have lost 10 per cent of their visitors. Reference sites have lost 15 per cent. Health sites have lost 31 per cent. For companies that sell advertising or subscriptions, lost visitors means lost revenue.

"We had a very positive relationship with Google for a long time... They broke the deal," says Neil Vogel, head of Dotdash Meredith, which owns titles such as People and Food & Wine. Three years ago its sites got more than 60 per cent of their traffic from Google. Now the figure is in the mid-30s. "They are stealing our content to compete with us," says Mr Vogel.

Google has insisted that its use of others' content is fair. But since it launched its AI overviews, the share of news-related searches resulting in no onward clicks has risen from 56 per cent to 69 per cent, estimates Similarweb. In other words, seven in ten people get their answer without visiting the page that supplied it.

"The nature of the internet has completely changed," says Prashanth Chandrasekar, chief executive of Stack Overflow, best known as an online forum for coders. "AI is basically choking off traffic to most content sites," he says. With fewer visitors, Stack Overflow is seeing fewer questions posted on its message boards. Wikipedia, also powered by enthusiasts, warns that AI-generated summaries without attribution "block pathways for people to access... and contribute to" the site.

To keep the traffic and the money coming, many big content producers have negotiated licensing deals with AI companies, backed up by legal threats: what Robert Thomson, chief executive of News Corp, has dubbed "wooing and suing". His company, which owns the Wall Street Journal and the New York Post, among other titles, has struck a deal with OpenAI. Two of its rivals, the New York Times and Axel Springer, have chosen to litigate instead.

AI companies have scraped far more content per user interaction than Google ever has — with even less return to creators. According to Cloudflare's analysis, the ratio of pages scraped to visitors sent was 2:1 for Google a decade ago; today it is 6:1. For OpenAI, it is 250:1; for Anthropic, 6,000:1.

Cloudflare CEO Matthew Prince outlines three possible scenarios for the web's future. The first is "content creator extinction" — where AI companies continue extracting content without compensation while traditional revenue streams disappear. The second is "AI oligarchy" — where five major AI companies vertically employ content creators, returning to a medieval patronage system. The third is a "Netflix model" — where AI companies compete through exclusive licensing agreements, similar to streaming platforms.

"The fuel that runs these AI engines is original content," Prince said. "So that content has to be created for these engines to work. And someone has to pay for it." The question is whether the open web can survive the rise of AI — or whether it will evolve into something very different.

🔍 长难句解析

"They told Mr Prince, whose firm, Cloudflare, provides security infrastructure to about a fifth of the web, that their businesses faced a grave new online threat."
结构分析：
主句是They told Mr Prince...that...，其中that引导宾语从句。whose firm...是非限制性定语从句修饰Mr Prince，插入语Cloudflare是同位语解释firm。

翻译：他们告诉Prince先生——他的公司Cloudflare为大约五分之一的互联网提供安全基础设施——他们的企业面临着严峻的新的网络威胁。
"As users pose their queries to chatbots rather than conventional search engines, they are given answers, rather than links to follow."
结构分析：
As引导时间状语从句，rather than表示"而不是"，出现两次形成平行结构。主句they are given answers是被动语态，rather than links to follow是比较状语。

翻译：当用户向聊天机器人而非传统搜索引擎提出查询时，他们得到的是答案，而不是可以点击进入的链接。
"The result is that 'content' publishers, from news providers and online forums to reference sites such as Wikipedia, are seeing alarming drops in their traffic."
结构分析：
主句The result is that...中that引导表语从句。from...to...是插入语，列举publisher的类型。such as Wikipedia是reference sites的同位语。

翻译：结果是，从新闻提供商、在线论坛到维基百科等参考网站的"内容"出版商，都看到他们的流量出现令人担忧的下降。
"Google has insisted that its use of others' content is fair, but since it launched its AI overviews, the share of news-related searches resulting in no onward clicks has risen from 56 per cent to 69 per cent."
结构分析：
but连接两个并列分句。第一个分句中that引导宾语从句。第二个分句中since引导时间状语从句，主句主语是the share...searches，resulting in...是现在分词短语作后置定语修饰searches。

翻译：谷歌坚称其对他人内容的使用是公平的，但自从推出AI概览功能以来，没有产生进一步点击的新闻相关搜索比例已从56%上升到69%。

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of this article?

正确答案：B
解析：文章开篇即点明主题——AI正在改变人们浏览网页的方式，进而改变互联网核心的经济交易模式。全文围绕内容创作者面临的流量下降、收入减少等困境展开，并探讨了可能的未来走向。A、C、D虽然在文中都有提及，但都是细节信息，不能概括全文主旨。

2. 【细节理解题】According to the article, what is the current ratio of pages scraped to visitors sent for different platforms?

正确答案：B
解析：根据文章倒数第三段"According to Cloudflare's analysis, the ratio of pages scraped to visitors sent was 2:1 for Google a decade ago; today it is 6:1. For OpenAI, it is 250:1; for Anthropic, 6,000:1." 注意题目问的是current ratio（当前比例），Google十年前是2:1，现在是6:1。D选项中的1,500:1和37,000:1是Prince在其他采访中提到的数据，不是本文内容。

3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred about Matthew Prince's view on the future of the web?

正确答案：C
解析：文章倒数第二段明确指出"Prince's preferred scenario positions AI companies as content aggregators competing primarily through exclusive licensing agreements, similar to streaming platforms"，并称之为"the third option, which I think is the most attractive one"。A选项与Prince的观点相反，他认为内容创作者灭绝是"super nihilistic outcome"且"pretty unlikely"。B选项错误，他认为寡头垄断是"a pretty scary outcome"。D选项与全文基调不符，Prince认为互联网商业模式必须改变才能生存。

💡 考研阅读技巧

  • 识别数字信息：考研阅读常出现大量数据，要注意区分不同时间点的数据（如Google十年前2:1 vs 现在6:1），以及不同主体的对比数据
  • 关注引语功能：文中引用多位人物的观点（Matthew Prince、Neil Vogel、Prashanth Chandrasekar等），引语往往直接支持作者论点，是答题关键
  • 把握文章结构：本文是典型的"提出问题-分析问题-展望未来"结构，开头用具体事例引入，中间用数据支撑，结尾给出解决方案
  • 词汇积累重点：本文中monetise、ubiquitous、oligarchy、patronage等是考研高频难词，需重点掌握
  • 注意比喻表达：如"the fuel that runs these AI engines"将内容比作燃料，"wooing and suing"用押头韵增强表达效果
