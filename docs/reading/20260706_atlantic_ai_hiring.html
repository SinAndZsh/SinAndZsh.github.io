AI Has Ruined the Job Market

Maybe flawed people were better than brute algorithms. · 外刊精读 · 2026年7月6日
话题分类：科技与社会 · 适合考研英语一/二

A few years ago, Ken Schumacher was working for a technology company. Part of his job involved assessing评估 v. potential hires: hopping on a Zoom call, giving an applicant an engineering test (kind of like a crossword puzzle with code instead of words), and going on "mute for an hour" as the applicant struggled努力 v./n. through it.

Except many of the candidates weren't struggling. The firm's exercises were getting posted on sites such as Glassdoor. "All these savvy精明的 adj. 23-year-olds would, of course, practice the problem three times, come to me, and crush it," Schumacher told me. "Now the bigger problem is everyone's using AI to write their resume." They're also using AI-powered chatbots聊天机器人 n. and teleprompters提词器 n. to help them get to the next round. It's become "really, really hard for anyone to figure out who's real and who's fake," Schumacher said. (This turned out to be its own market opportunity—he now runs a start-up using AI to detect AI cheating by job candidates.)

People had once hoped that Silicon Valley might not only smooth out the logistics of getting a job but also make the process fairer. Unbiased tools would replace alma-mater母校 n. networks. Digital portals would accept applications from anyone, anywhere. Workers would get free access to templates, practice exams, and advice. "Technology in general tends to improve the efficiency效率 n. of job matching," Mitchell Hoffman, a labor economist at UC Santa Barbara, told me. But AI in particular seems to destroy it. Employers and employees are locked in an "arms race, where it's AI-on-AI crime," Kathleen Creel, a philosopher and computer scientist at Northeastern University, told me.

In just a few years, tools such as ChatGPT and Claude have commoditized使商品化 v. the production of cover letters and resumes. Large shares of job applicants are using generative chatbots to polish their language and summarize总结 v. their accomplishments—raising the average quality of these personal documents, at the cost of "compressing" and "homogenizing使同质化 v." the information they convey, as one Columbia Business School paper put it. In Silicon Valley, the phenomenon is sometimes called "signal collapse." CVs used to be filled with advertent and inadvertent无意的 adj. signals for hiring managers to parse解析；分析 v.: degrees and certifications and languages spoken, as well as formatting errors and unusual digressions and over-honest admissions. Because AI has reduced the time job seekers spend writing applications, people are submitting hundreds of them on ZipRecruiter, LinkedIn, and other sites—often for positions that are not quite a fit, often never to hear anything back. Applicants aren't getting feedback on their strengths and weaknesses. They're not getting intel情报；信息 n. on what they might need to do to get hired, or the skills they might need to obtain the job they want. Signal collapse is running in both directions.

A recent survey by Resume Builder found that four in five companies are using AI to scan resumes, two in five are using chatbots to communicate with candidates, and one in five is giving AI interviews. AI screening tools in particular are creating an "algorithmic算法的 adj. monoculture单一文化 n." in hiring, a new study of 4 million job applications has found. A decade or three ago, firms would "go through the resumes and pick out schools they knew, or fancy schools, or schools that had good programs" in relevant fields, Creel told me. "That's bad in its own way, from an egalitarian平等主义的 adj. point of view. But at least different people had different arbitrary screening criteria."

Moreover, screening algorithms are discriminating against Black and Asian candidates, the paper found. "There's this filtering happening, and we don't understand it, because these systems are opaque不透明的 adj. and custom-built for different institutions, and we don't know the functionality功能 n.," Sarah Bana, a co-author of the paper and a digital fellow at the Stanford Digital Economy Lab, told me. Nevertheless, the algorithms' tendency to advantage the advantaged and disadvantage the disadvantaged seems clear.

Companies ask applicants to describe how they would respond to an unruly不守规矩的 adj. customer, or figure out an engineering fix to a software problem. A number of large firms are making face-to-face interactions a bigger part of the hiring process again. Google is ensuring that candidates have "at least one round" of in-person interviews, to "make sure the fundamentals are there," Sundar Pichai, its CEO, has said. Cisco is increasing "verification steps and enhanced background checks that may involve an in-person component." Companies are also extending probationary试用期的 adj. periods and hiring people on contract, with the intention of turning them into full-time employees after managers can vet审查 v. their work.

They aren't doing this because "the Harvard engineers are the best in the world," he said. "But it's the safer play in an era where it's really hard to tell who is legit名副其实的 adj. and who is kind of full of crap."

📋 文章大意总结

本文发表于《大西洋月刊》，探讨了人工智能如何从根本上扰乱了招聘流程。文章以科技从业者Schumacher的亲身经历开篇，揭示了AI工具在招聘中的滥用现象：求职者使用AI编写简历、生成面试答案，甚至通过提词器作弊。作者指出AI已将招聘"Tinder化"（大量投递却无回应）和"Amazon化"（抹除独特性、充斥同质化内容），导致核心概念"信号崩溃"——所有求职者看起来都同样优秀，招聘方难以区分真伪。文章进一步指出，企业为筛选海量AI生成的简历而转用AI筛选工具，却造成了"算法单一文化"，甚至对少数族裔候选人产生歧视。最终，AI非但未提升效率，反而迫使企业回归面对面面试、延长试用期、依赖校友推荐等传统方式。作者警告，整个劳动力市场可能因此变得更加僵化，企业活力减退。文章以一句幽默建议收尾：在求职信中做真实的自己，并亲自递交——也许是这个AI时代最好的求职策略。

🔍 长难句解析

Sentence 1: AI has Tinderized hiring. Workers are applying to hundreds of positions and never hearing back; companies are receiving thousands of resumes and struggling to respond. More than that, AI has Amazoned hiring. It has scrubbed the uniqueness out of applications, flooded the market with same-same-seeming offerings, increased the number of frauds, and replaced personal discretion with brute algorithmic assessment.

【结构分析】 这组句子使用了两个精彩的类比动词"Tinderized"和"Amazoned"作谓语。最后一个长句包含五个并列的谓语结构：scrubbed、flooded、increased、replaced，形成排比效果，增强语势。

【修辞分析】 作者将招聘比作Tinder（约会软件：海量匹配但多数无果）和Amazon（电商平台：商品同质化），生动形象地描述了AI对招聘的双重破坏。

【参考译文】 AI把招聘变成了Tinder式体验——求职者投递数百份简历却石沉大海，企业收到成千上万份简历却无力应对。不仅如此，AI还把招聘变成了Amazon模式：它抹除了申请中的独特性，用千篇一律的内容淹没市场，增加了欺诈数量，用冰冷的算法评估取代了人的判断力。

Sentence 2: Large shares of job applicants are using generative chatbots to polish their language and summarize their accomplishments—raising the average quality of these personal documents, at the cost of "compressing" and "homogenizing" the information they convey, as one Columbia Business School paper put it.

【结构分析】 主句：applicants are using chatbots to polish... and summarize...；破折号后的现在分词短语 raising... 作结果状语；介词短语 at the cost of "compressing" and "homogenizing" 补充说明代价；as引导的从句为插入语，引出信息来源。

【词汇解析】 polish 润色；compress 压缩；homogenize 使同质化。三个动词语义递进，揭示了AI写作工具的双刃剑效应：表面质量提升，实质信息压缩。

【参考译文】 大量求职者正在使用生成式聊天机器人来润色语言和总结成就——正如哥伦比亚商学院一篇论文所指出的，这提高了个人文档的平均质量，代价却是"压缩"和"同质化"了它们所传达的信息。

Sentence 3: The result is that AI is slowing HR down instead of speeding it up, because companies have to spend more time scrutinizing applications and performing background checks.

【结构分析】 主句主干：The result is that...；that引导表语从句，其中包含instead of构成的对比结构（slowing down vs. speeding up）；because引导原因状语从句，解释为何AI反而降低了效率。

【逻辑分析】 此句揭示了文章的核心悖论：AI本应提高效率，实际上却适得其反。这种"技术反噬"的逻辑在考研阅读推理判断题中是高频考点。

【参考译文】 结果是AI非但没有加快人力资源的运转速度，反而使其放慢了，因为公司不得不花更多时间仔细审查申请和进行背景调查。

🎓 考研阅读技巧

1. 类比动词理解

文中"Tinderized"和"Amazoned"是将品牌名转化为动词的修辞手法。考研阅读中遇到此类词，需根据上下文推断其引申含义——不必纠结字面意思。

2. 引号功能判断

"signal collapse"、"algorithmic monoculture"、"arms race"等带引号的术语往往是作者引入的核心概念，也是命题高频区域。注意引号内容与上下文的逻辑关系。

3. 反讽语气识别

"retreating to pedigree"以幽默讽刺的方式揭示AI招聘的悖论。考研英语中作者的嘲讽语气常通过看似正面实则反面的表达来体现。

4. 专家观点对应

文章引用多位专家观点（Schumacher, Hoffman, Creel, Bana）。做细节题时需注意将选项与具体人名的观点进行精确匹配，避免张冠李戴。

5. 悖论逻辑把握

文章核心悖论：AI本应提高效率，却适得其反。考研推理判断题常考查"作者认为技术带来了什么意想不到的后果"，抓住悖论即抓住答案。

6. 因果链追踪

AI→简历同质化→企业用AI筛选→算法偏见→企业回归传统招聘→劳动力市场僵化。理清这条因果链有助于快速定位信息、判断段落关系。

1. Which of the following best describes the central idea of the passage?
  • A. AI has created new job opportunities that benefit both employers and employees
  • B. AI has paradoxically made hiring less efficient by destroying genuine signals and forcing companies to revert to old methods
  • C. Algorithmic screening tools are the ultimate solution to the inefficiencies of traditional hiring
  • D. Job seekers should embrace AI tools to gain competitive advantages in the labor market
正确答案：B
解析：这是一道主旨大意题。文章的核心论点是AI非但没有提高招聘效率，反而造成了"信号崩溃"——抹平了求职者差异、增加了欺诈，迫使企业回归面对面面试和校友推荐等传统方式，甚至可能使整个劳动力市场更加僵化。A与文章主旨相反（AI并未创造双赢）；C与文章观点矛盾（算法筛选造成了"算法单一文化"甚至种族歧视）；D并非文章主旨（文章对求职者使用AI持批评态度）。B准确概括了"AI适得其反"的核心悖论。
2. What does the author mean by the phrase "algorithmic monoculture" (Paragraph 7)?
  • A. A diverse range of AI tools used by different companies for hiring
  • B. The tendency of AI screening tools to produce identical hiring outcomes across firms
  • C. A unified global standard for ethical AI recruitment practices
  • D. The increasing use of AI by a single dominant technology company
正确答案：B
解析：这是一道词义猜测题。根据上下文，"AI screening tools in particular are creating an 'algorithmic monoculture' in hiring"，紧接着解释"More candidates are being rejected by every firm that they apply to; in general, hiring decisions are more uniform than they would be if HR managers were not using algorithmic screens." 这说明"算法单一文化"指的是AI筛选工具导致各公司的招聘决策趋于一致、缺乏多样性，即B选项所述。monoculture本意为"单一栽培"，在此引申为"决策同质化"。
3. What can be inferred from the passage about companies' responses to AI-driven hiring challenges?
  • A. Most companies have abandoned AI tools entirely and returned to manual hiring processes
  • B. Companies are increasing their reliance on AI while simultaneously introducing more rigorous human oversight
  • C. Companies are adopting a combination of traditional methods and enhanced verification measures to cope with AI disruptions
  • D. Companies believe that AI will eventually solve the current hiring inefficiencies on its own
正确答案：C
解析：这是一道推理判断题。文章倒数第三段提到Google确保"至少一轮"线下面试、Cisco增加验证步骤和背景调查、企业延长试用期、以及最后一段提到企业诉诸推荐、校友网络、猎头等传统方式。这些措施是传统方法与增强验证手段的结合，正是C选项所概括的。A过于绝对（文章说"making face-to-face interactions a bigger part"，而非"abandoned AI entirely"）；B不准确（企业并未"increasing reliance on AI"，而是转向传统方式）；D与文章矛盾（作者暗示AI短期内无法自行解决这些问题）。
