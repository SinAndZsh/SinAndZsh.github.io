AI Has Created a Cheating Crisis. Universities Must Change.

人工智能引发大学作弊危机，高校必须改变评估方式

来源：The New York Times 原文日期：July 14, 2026 精读生成：2026-07-19 话题：教育 / 科技

文章大意

本文出自《纽约时报》2026年7月14日评论版，作者为纽约大学副校长克莱·舍基（Clay Shirky）。文章指出，生成式人工智能已在大学生中造成普遍性作弊危机：即便是最积极好学的学生，也开始用 ChatGPT 等工具完成课后作业。作者认为，简单的 AI 使用规范或检测工具无法解决问题，因为 AI 检测器误报率高，而学生只需轻微编辑就能规避检测。真正的出路是重新设计大学评估方式——从课后论文转向课堂写作、口头答辩、苏格拉底式对话和“真实评估”。作者进一步以中世纪大学 oral examination 的历史为例，说明书面作业并非高等教育的古老传统，当前变革只是回归一种更古老、更人际化的教育模式。大学不应被视为信息传递机构，而是塑造学生身份与思维习惯的场所。

阅读技巧提示

  • 本文是典型的“问题—解决方案”结构：先指出 AI 作弊危机，再提出评估方式改革，最后展望教育的未来形态。阅读时注意这一主线。
  • 作者多次使用历史对比（中世纪大学 → 19 世纪德国研究型大学 → 二战后美国通识教育），帮助读者理解“书面作业并非自古不变”。
  • 遇到 philosophy professor, vice provost, freshman comp 等学术场景词汇时，结合上下文推断其角色与功能。
  • 词义猜测题注意同义替换：如 active engagement 与 lazy uses 形成反义对照；information-transfer 与 identity-formation 构成概念对比。
  • 长难句常含插入语、同位语或分号连接的并列结构，先抓主干再处理修饰成分。

The Medieval Roots of Assessment

Talking, listening, and reading have been part of academic culture from the beginning, but written assignments—the classic five-paragraph essay, the research paper, the critical reading essay—have not. 源于；来自 the Latin verb “lego,” which commonly means “to read.” In the medieval university, lectures consisted of professors reading a book to students, sometimes the only copy the institution owned. Sometimes professors added their own comments to the reading; sometimes they did not. Some students had to write down what they heard; others simply had to listen. At times, writing was actively discouraged. In 1355, the Faculty of Arts at the University of Paris prohibitedv. /prəˈhɪbɪtɪd/
禁止 masters from lecturing at a slow enough pace that students could transcribe their words.

Which tasks are written and which should be oral is something that has changed over the centuries. What the Future Classroom Looks Like

Although the return of the in-class written essay is one sign of this shift, a range of old practices is being revived to assess student learning. Professors can engage students in conversation, through Socratic dialogue or simple question-and-answer. They can talk with students directly or have them pose questions to one another. This may require device-free classrooms, since many professors have also reported students using ChatGPT in class itself to craft answers to questions asked in the room.

Professors can require students to attend in-person office hours, have similar unscripted interactions, or ask them to perform tasks they can only carry out if they have actually learned the material—what is called authentic assessmentn. /ɔːˈθentɪk əˈsesmənt/

There are also tools to monitor or lock browsers remotely to prevent students from using AI while working, even when they are not in class. Some schools are building internet-free rooms for exams. The goal is for students to show what they have internalizedv. /ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪzd/

1. What is the main argument of the article?

  • A. Universities should ban AI completely to restore academic honesty.
  • B. AI detectors are reliable tools for identifying cheating students.
  • C. The AI cheating crisis requires universities to redesign how they assess student learning.
  • D. Written essays should remain the dominant form of university assessment.

正确答案：C

解析：文章开篇指出 AI 作弊已成危机，随后论证简单的 AI 规范或检测工具无效，并提出高校应转向课堂写作、口头答辩、真实评估等方式。A 项“全面禁止 AI”并非作者观点；B 项与原文“检测器不可靠”相反；D 项与作者主张的“减少课后书面作业”相悖。

2. Why does the author think AI detectors are not a good solution to the cheating crisis?

  • A. They are too expensive for most universities to afford.
  • B. They tend to falsely accuse students and fail to catch edited AI output.
  • C. Students do not know how to use them properly.
  • D. They encourage students to use AI more creatively.

正确答案：B

解析：原文明确提到：AI 检测工具“too prone to false accusations of cheating and too poor at detecting lightly edited output”，即误报率高且难以识别经过轻微编辑的 AI 文本。A、C、D 三项在文中均未提及或与文意不符。

3. What can be inferred from the author’s view that “the university is not in the information-transfer business; it is in the identity-formation business”?

  • A. Universities should focus on transmitting facts rather than shaping students’ character.
  • B. The value of higher education lies mainly in transforming who students become, not just what they know.
  • C. Online courses will soon replace traditional universities entirely.
  • D. Students should rely on AI to acquire information more efficiently.

正确答案：B

解析：作者通过对比 information-transfer 与 identity-formation，强调大学的核心价值不仅在于传递信息，更在于塑造学生的身份、思维习惯与学习能力。A 项与文意相反；C 项过度推断；D 项与作者反对学生依赖 AI 的立场不符。
