AI Homework 'Help' Is Harming Student Learning, Major Study Finds

Research on 26,811 students reveals a stark gap between AI-assisted homework gains and plummeting exam performance

Students who rely on generative artificial intelligence to complete their assignments may be trading short-term convenience for long-term academic failure, according to groundbreaking research published by the Centre for Economic Policy Research. The study, which tracked 26,811 students in grades seven through 12 over 30 months, reveals a starkadj. 明显的；荒凉的 divergence between homework performance and actual learning retention, suggesting that AI "outsourcing" is creating a generation of learners who master the tool but fail the subject.

The findings indicate that while AI adoption boosted homework scores by 18% and reduced completion time by nearly a third, the academic gains were largely illusoryadj. 虚假的；幻觉的. Within six months, the same students saw their monthly exam scores drop by 20%. The impact was even more pronounced on high-stakes college entrance exams, where scores plummetedv. 暴跌；坠落 by 18% to 24%, with results reaching their lowest point after two years of consistent AI reliance.

Researchers from Stockholm University and the University of Hong Kong highlighted a phenomenon known as the "transfern./v. 转移；迁移；转学 problem." This occurs when a student becomes proficient at using a specific device or software to produce an answer but remains unable to generalize that knowledge or apply it independently during a supervised examination. "For students, completing these tasks efficiently is not the goal; learning from them is," the researchers wrote. "Our findings show that generative AI, which is likely to become a prevalentadj. 流行的；普遍的 technology for education, has a substantial negative impact on student learning."

The study identified a specific student profile most at risk: those who "outsourced" their homework, deploying AI to complete assignments accurately but with minimal personal engagement. Approximately 80% of these students experienced significant declines in test performance. The emergence of this profile not only exposes a dissonancen. 不一致；不和谐音 in AI productivity versus actual learning gains—it fuels an argument many educators have made against the unfetteredadj. 不受限制的；自由的 use of technology in education.

This struggle with knowledge transfer is not a new challenge in the history of education. In 1924, Ohio State University professor Sidney Pressey debuted the "teaching machine," a mechanical device intended to automate drills. Three decades later, the legendary behaviorist B.F. Skinner refined the concept. Both projects were eventually abandoned by their creators because, while students could navigate the machines perfectly, they could not demonstrate the same level of masteryn. 精通；掌握；控制 when the technology was removed. In a letter to Skinner, Pressey conceded that while students had not mastered the subject matter, they had "mastered the machine."

"The reason they all quit was the transfer problem," explained neuroscientist Jared Cooney Horvath in testimony to the U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation. "They found that kids would be very good so long as they were using the tool, but as soon as they went off the tool, they couldn't do it anymore." Horvath argues that AI has the potential to once again recreate the problems of the teaching machine, fostering dependencyn. 依赖；从属 rather than expertise.

The scale of adoption suggests this is no longer a nichen. 小众市场；壁龛 adj. 小众的 issue. According to a survey by CollegeBoard, approximately 84% of high school students in the United States have used AI for homework. This widespread usage is often driven by more than just a desire to cheat; it is frequently fueled by intense economic anxiety. Data from the job platform Monster shows that nearly 90% of the graduating class of 2026 expresses concern that AI and automation will eliminate entry-level positions, creating a high-pressure environment where students feel they must use the technology to remain competitiveadj. 竞争的；有竞争力的.

"AI is going to replace them, at least a lot of them, and they know that, and we're pretending that it won't," said Jacob Shelley, an associate professor at Western University, who has observed a significant anomalyn. 异常；反常事物 in recent testing where students achieved perfect scores on multiple-choice sections—likely through AI-assisted shortcuts—but failed to produce coherent arguments in essay portions that required curriculum-specific knowledge. "Students are responsible, but I don't really blame them here."

While some proponents of educational technology argue that AI can be used for "scaffoldingn. 脚手架；支撑；支架式教学"—tailoring content to a student's specific reading level—critics warn that using expert-level tools as a novice prevents the development of foundational skills. "The tools experts use to make their lives easier are not the tools children should use to learn how to become experts," Horvath argued. "When you use offloading tools that experts use to make their lives easier as a novice, as a student, you don't learn the skill. You simply learn dependency."

The researchers concluded that the rapid diffusion of these tools is having a "substantial negative impact" on student learning, as the primary goal of homework—the process of learning through productive struggle—is being replaced by the goal of efficient task completion. As educators and policymakers grapplev. 努力解决；扭打 with these findings, the question is no longer whether AI will be part of education, but how to ensure it serves learning rather than underminingv. 破坏；削弱；暗中损害 it.

阅读理解练习
1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of this article?
A. Chinese students perform worse than American students in high-stakes examinations.
B. A major study reveals that relying on AI for homework boosts short-term scores but significantly damages long-term learning and exam performance.
C. All educational technology should be banned from schools to protect student learning.
D. Economic anxiety is the primary reason why students choose to attend university.
正确答案：B
解析：本文围绕CEPR的一项大规模研究展开，核心论点是使用AI完成作业虽然短期提高了作业分数（+18%），但长期来看严重损害了学生的学习能力和考试成绩（月考-20%，高考-18%~24%）。A选项偏离主题（文章并非比较中美学生）；C选项过于极端（文章并未呼吁禁止所有教育技术）；D选项以偏概全（经济焦虑只是学生使用AI的动机之一，不是文章主旨）。
2. 【细节理解题】According to the article, what does the term "transfer problem" refer to?
A. Students transferring from one school to another due to poor grades.
B. The inability to transfer files between different AI software programs.
C. Students mastering AI tools but failing to apply knowledge independently without technological assistance.
D. The financial transfer of educational funds from public to private institutions.
正确答案：C
解析：根据文章第三段，"transfer problem"指的是"a student becomes proficient at using a specific device or software to produce an answer but remains unable to generalize that knowledge or apply it independently"（学生精通使用特定设备或软件生成答案，但仍无法泛化知识或独立应用）。这是一个自1924年教学机器时代就存在的教育心理学概念。A、B、D均为对"transfer"的字面误解，属于考研阅读常见的干扰项设置手法。
3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred about Professor Jacob Shelley's attitude toward students using AI for homework?
A. He believes students should be severely punished for using AI in any academic context.
B. He is completely indifferent to the issue and sees it as a natural technological evolution.
C. He understands why students turn to AI but remains concerned about its negative impact on genuine learning.
D. He advocates for complete integration of AI into all aspects of university education.
正确答案：C
解析：根据文章倒数第三段，Shelley教授一方面承认"Students are responsible"（学生有责任），但紧接着说"I don't really blame them here"（但我并不真正责怪他们），并解释了学生面临的就业焦虑（"AI is going to replace them... they know that, and we're pretending that it won't"）。同时，他在前文也指出了AI使用导致的测试异常现象（anomaly）。这表明他理解学生使用AI的动机，但仍担忧其对真正学习的影响。A过于严厉；B和D与文章观点完全相反。

阅读技巧提示
🎯 技巧一：数据类文章的关注重点
本文属于典型的研究报道类文章，阅读时应重点关注：研究对象（26,811名中学生）、核心发现（作业+18% vs 考试-20%）、时间跨度（30个月）、历史类比（1924年教学机器）以及专家观点。这些数据往往是出题点。
🎯 技巧二：识别"引号特殊用法"
考研阅读中，引号常表示特殊含义、反讽或术语。如本文中"outsourcing"（外包）加引号，并非真正的商业外包，而是指学生将作业"外包"给AI；"help"在标题中也是反讽用法。识别这些修辞能帮助你准确把握作者态度。
🎯 技巧三：对比结构的快速理解
本文多次使用对比结构：short-term vs long-term、homework gains vs exam losses、mastering the tool vs mastering the subject。抓住这些对比关系，可以快速理清文章论证逻辑。
🎯 技巧四：专家引语的作用
文中引用多位专家（Horvath、Shelley、研究者）的观点，都是为了支撑核心论点：AI依赖损害学习。阅读时可以快速判断：引语是支持还是反驳前文观点？这有助于把握文章整体走向。
