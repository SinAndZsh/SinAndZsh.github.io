📋 文章大意

本文报道了超过250位美国企业CEO联名在《纽约时报》发表公开信，呼吁全美各州将人工智能（AI）和计算机科学列为K-12（幼儿园至高中）教育的核心必修课程。签署者包括微软CEO萨蒂亚·纳德拉、Uber CEO达拉·科斯罗萨西等商业巨头。文章指出，此举是"保持美国全球竞争力"的关键，并警告美国正在落后于已将相关课程设为必修课的中国、巴西、韩国、新加坡等国家。文章还援引布鲁金斯学会的研究数据，显示学习计算机科学的学生毕业后薪资平均高出8%。同时，文章也提及了部分教育工作者对AI可能削弱学生批判性思维的担忧。

Over 250 CEOs Urge Schools to Make AI and Computer Science Core Subjects

More than 250 American chief executives have joined together in an open letter published in The New York Times, urging states across the nation to make artificial intelligence and computer science mandatory subjects in K-12 education. The signatories include high-profile executives such as Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella, Etsy CEO Josh Silverman, and Uber CEO Dara Khosrowshahi. Together, they describe a widening skills gap and say computer science education is critical to "keeping America competitive."

"Countries like Brazil, China, South Korea, and Singapore have already made computer science or AI a compulsory part of their school curriculum," the letter states. "Meanwhile, only 12 U.S. states require high school students to take computer science courses to graduate." The executives argue that in the age of artificial intelligence, America must prepare its children for the future by making them "creators of AI, not just consumers."

The letter poses a thought-provoking question: "What if one course could help close the wage gap, unlock $660 billion in annual economic potential, and address the skills chasm?" The answer, according to the executives, is to integrate AI and computer science into the nation's foundational education system. They emphasize that basic knowledge of computer science and AI is essential for students to "thrive in a technology-driven world."

The timing of this letter is significant. It comes as the Trump administration is pushing its American AI strategy. In late April, President Trump signed an executive order establishing the "White House Task Force on AI Education," aimed at expanding AI instruction in K-12 through partnerships between industry, academia, and government. However, these efforts are occurring against a backdrop of significant cuts to federal education funding. In March, the Department of Education underwent a massive staff reduction of nearly 50%, and education funding has been substantially reduced. Educators have expressed deep concern about these developments—not only for the future of students but for their own careers as well.

On the private sector side, companies are stepping in to fill the gap. According to Cameron Wilson, president of Code.org, "We provide free curriculum resources to teachers and build sustainable training systems." The organization has already made its computer science and AI courses freely available to teachers nationwide, covering programming fundamentals, algorithmic thinking, and AI ethics. Wilson emphasizes that "true AI literacy is not just about using tools, but understanding how they work and learning to solve problems with technology."

Research from the Brookings Institution provides compelling evidence for the economic benefits of computer science education. Their latest study shows that high school students who take computer science courses earn an average of 8% more after graduation compared to those who do not. This gap is even more pronounced among women, students from low-income families, and Black students. The study also notes that since the 2016 AP Computer Science curriculum reform, the number of female, Black, and Hispanic students taking the exam has increased fourfold.

"This is not just an education issue; it is key to closing the intergenerational skills and income gap," the letter emphasizes. Taking Alabama as an example, since the state made computer science a graduation requirement in 2023, college applications for related majors have increased by 15%, and local employment in technology positions has grown significantly.

As of early May, 12 states—including Alabama, Arkansas, and Indiana—have made computer science a mandatory requirement for high school graduation. West Virginia became the latest state to join this group when it passed related legislation last week. Code.org data shows that by 2024, 35 states will have established computer science education plans, up from just 18 states in 2020 and 2 states in 2017. Additionally, 22 states now allow computer science courses to substitute for traditional math, science, or foreign language credits, further incentivizing students to enroll.

However, challenges remain. According to the Computer Science Teachers Association, only 6.4% of high school students currently elect to take computer science courses. "Many students never try computer science because it is not a graduation requirement," the letter criticizes. "If a single course could help close the wage gap, unlock hundreds of billions in economic potential, and address the skills shortage, wouldn't we want every student to have access to it?"

Despite the strong push from business leaders, some educators have raised concerns about potential negative effects of AI education. Some teachers worry that over-reliance on AI tools could erode students' critical thinking and problem-solving abilities. In response, Wilson argues that "AI instruction should focus on 'scaffolded knowledge'—starting with using tools, gradually delving into principles, and ultimately fostering innovation capabilities." He cites Code.org's curriculum design, which includes modules on "AI ethics debates" and "algorithmic bias analysis," aiming to guide students in critical thinking.

The deeper motivation behind this corporate initiative is to address the skills gap. The signatories of the letter span technology and non-technology companies including American Express, Airbnb, Dropbox, and Salesforce, highlighting the universal demand for AI skills. A recent Microsoft study found that many newly hired programmers rely on AI tools to generate code but have only a superficial understanding of the underlying logic. "When the system fails, they don't know how to fix it," the study notes. This skills gap is precisely what the executives hope to address through early education.

The letter concludes with a call to action: "We stand at a pivotal moment in history. The decisions we make today about education will determine America's competitiveness for decades to come. We urge every state to make computer science and AI education a priority—not as an elective, but as a fundamental part of every student's education."

🔍 长难句解析

"What if one course could help close the wage gap, unlock $660 billion in annual economic potential, and address the skills chasm?"
结构分析：
这是一个What if引导的虚拟条件句，表示"如果……会怎样"。主语是one course，谓语包含三个并列的动词短语：help close the wage gap（帮助缩小工资差距）、unlock $660 billion in annual economic potential（释放每年6600亿美元的经济潜力）、address the skills chasm（解决技能鸿沟）。

翻译：如果一门课程就能帮助缩小工资差距、释放每年6600亿美元的经济潜力，并解决技能鸿沟，会怎样？
"The timing of this letter is significant, coming as the Trump administration is pushing its American AI strategy."
结构分析：
主句是The timing of this letter is significant。coming as...是现在分词短语作伴随状语，其中as引导时间状语从句，表示"正当……的时候"。

翻译：这封信的时机意义重大，正值特朗普政府推进其美国人工智能战略之际。
结构分析：
主语是A recent Microsoft study，谓语是found，that引导宾语从句。宾语从句中，many newly hired programmers是主语，rely on AI tools to generate code和have only a superficial understanding of the underlying logic是并列谓语。

翻译：微软最近的一项研究发现，许多新入职的程序员依赖AI工具生成代码，但对底层逻辑只有肤浅的理解。
"This is not just an education issue; it is key to closing the intergenerational skills and income gap."
结构分析：
分号连接两个并列句。第一句主系表结构，第二句中key to...表示"……的关键"，其中to是介词，后接动名词短语closing the intergenerational skills and income gap。

翻译：这不仅仅是教育问题；它是缩小代际技能和收入差距的关键。

✏ 阅读理解练习

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main purpose of the open letter signed by over 250 CEOs?

正确答案：B
解析：文章开篇即指出"More than 250 American chief executives have joined together in an open letter... urging states across the nation to make artificial intelligence and computer science mandatory subjects in K-12 education"，明确说明公开信的主要目的是敦促各州将AI和计算机科学列为K-12教育的必修课。A、C、D虽然在文中有所提及，但都不是主要目的。

2. 【细节理解题】According to the Brookings Institution study mentioned in the article, which of the following statements is TRUE?

正确答案：C
解析：文章明确提到"Their latest study shows that high school students who take computer science courses earn an average of 8% more after graduation compared to those who do not"，与选项C完全一致。A与原文矛盾；B错误，原文说女性、低收入家庭和黑人学生的差距"更为显著"；D文中未提及。

3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred about the author's attitude toward making AI and computer science mandatory?

正确答案：B
解析：作者在文章中既呈现了CEO们支持将AI和计算机科学列为必修课的观点和论据（如经济收益、国际竞争等），也提到了教育工作者的担忧（"some educators have raised concerns about potential negative effects"，担心AI会"erode students' critical thinking"）。这种平衡的报道方式体现了作者的中立态度。A和C过于片面，D与文章主旨相悖。

💡 考研阅读技巧

  • 注意数字信息：本文包含大量数据（12 states, 8% increase, 660 billion, 6.4%等），考研阅读中数字往往是考点，要准确定位
  • 识别引用结构：文章中多次出现直接引语（"...", the letter states/note/emphasizes），这些引用常用来支持论点，是理解作者态度的关键
  • 关注对比论证：文章大量使用对比（美国vs其他国家、支持方vs担忧方），这是考研英语常见的论证手法
  • 掌握专业词汇：本文涉及教育政策领域，curriculum, mandatory, compulsory, elective等词汇是考研高频词
  • 注意同义替换：mandatory/compulsory/required, gap/chasm, basic/fundamental等是同义替换的常见形式
