Big Tech Wants AI in the Classroom. These Teachers are Pushing Back. 难度：★★★★☆ 1200+ 词

科技巨头争相将人工智能引入课堂，但教师们对此心存疑虑

When I asked Eric Colburn, an English teacher at Brookline High School, how many students at BHS are using AI, he didn't hesitate: "Close to 100%." "Of course, it's hard to know for sure," he added with a smile. Mr. Colburn's estimate sounded high – or maybe I just couldn't believe all these 16-year-olds were more tech savvy/tek ˈsævi/ adj. 精通技术的 than me. But the research suggests he's not far off.

The Cypress, Brookline High School's student news site, surveyed the student body last November and found that 3 in 4四分之三 students reported using AI for schoolwork. That same month, Gemini and Notebook LM were made available to all students for free through their Google Suite, meaning usage rates have likely climbed/klaɪmd/ v. 攀升，上升 since then.

"By the end of last year, I realized my students couldn't do an assignment without AI now. They would fail if I told them to do it without AI," Peter Sedlack, an English teacher at Brookline High School, told me. "I think some of them, within the year, had lost the ability to actually do the work." This revelation/ˌrevəˈleɪʃn/ n. 启示，揭露 points to a deeper concern: the erosion/ɪˈrəʊʒn/ n. 侵蚀，削弱 of fundamental cognitive skills when AI becomes a crutch/krʌtʃ/ n. 拐杖，依赖.

Nationally, school districts have done a sharp about-face/əˈbaʊt feɪs/ n. 彻底改变，大转变 on AI since 2023, with adoption/əˈdɒpʃn/ n. 采用，采纳 rates surging/ˈsɜːdʒɪŋ/ adj. 激增的 in 2025. New York City was among the first to ban/bæn/ v. 禁止 the technology outright in early 2023, citing/ˈsaɪtɪŋ/ v. 引用，引证 concerns over cheating and harm to student learning. By the end of that year, the district had reversed/rɪˈvɜːst/ v. 逆转，反转 course, lifting the ban and launching an AI Policy Lab to guide its approach to integrating/ˈɪntɪɡreɪtɪŋ/ v. 整合，融合 the technology.

In 2025, Miami-Dade County Public Schools, the nation's third-largest school system, rolled out/rəʊld aʊt/ phr. 推出，展开 Google's Gemini for more than 100,000 high school students. Broward County, the nation's sixth-largest district, introduced Microsoft's Copilot for thousands of teachers. Many other districts have followed, experimenting/ɪkˈsperɪmentɪŋ/ v. 尝试，实验 with custom AI platforms and personal assistants for teachers and students, with mixed results/mɪkst rɪˈzʌlts/ phr. 结果喜忧参半.

However, beneath these statistics/stəˈtɪstɪks/ n. 统计数据 lies a more nuanced/ˈnjuːɑːnst/ adj. 微妙的，细致的 reality. Teachers report that AI use among students is not always productive/prəˈdʌktɪv/ adj. 富有成效的. "Students are using AI to write essays, but they're not learning how to write," says Maria Santos, a veteran English teacher with 20 years of experience. "When I ask them to explain their reasoning, they draw a blank/drɔː ə blæŋk/ phr. 大脑一片空白."

The paradox/ˈpærədɒks/ n. 悖论，矛盾 of AI in education is that while it promises to personalize/ˈpɜːsənəlaɪz/ v. 个性化 learning and free teachers from administrative/ədˈmɪnɪstrətɪv/ adj. 行政的，管理的 drudgery/ˈdrʌdʒəri/ n. 苦差事，单调乏味的工作, it also risks undermining/ˌʌndəˈmaɪnɪŋ/ v. 削弱，破坏 the very skills education aims to develop. As one teacher succinctly/səkˈsɪŋktli/ adv. 简洁地，简明地 put it: "We're teaching students to be prompt/prɒmpt/ n. 提示词，指令 engineers, not critical thinkers."

The implications/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃnz/ n. 影响，含义 extend beyond individual classrooms. If students graduate unable to write coherent/kəʊˈhɪərənt/ adj. 连贯的，条理清晰的 essays or articulate/ɑːˈtɪkjuleɪt/ v. 清晰表达 complex ideas without AI assistance, what does this mean for higher education and the workforce? Universities are already reporting that incoming/ˈɪnkʌmɪŋ/ adj. 新来的，刚入学的 students lack basic writing proficiency/prəˈfɪʃnsi/ n. 熟练，精通.

Some educators are pushing back. At Brookline High, a group of teachers has proposed a "AI-free curriculum/kəˈrɪkjələm/ n. 课程" for certain assignments, requiring students to complete work without digital assistance. "We need to preserve/prɪˈzɜːv/ v. 保护，保留 the space for intellectual/ˌɪntəˈlektʃuəl/ adj. 智力的，知识的 struggle," argues Dr. James Morrison, the school's curriculum director. "That's where real learning happens."

The debate over AI in education is ultimately a debate about what education is for. If the goal is efficiency/ɪˈfɪʃnsi/ n. 效率 and standardized/ˈstændədaɪzd/ adj. 标准化的 outcomes, AI is an unparalleled/ʌnˈpærəleld/ adj. 无与伦比的 tool. But if the goal is to develop independent thinkers capable of navigating/ˈnævɪɡeɪtɪŋ/ v. 驾驭，应对 an uncertain future, we may need to be more circumspect/ˈsɜːkəmspekt/ adj. 谨慎的，小心的 about how we deploy/dɪˈplɔɪ/ v. 部署，运用 this technology in our classrooms.

文章大意总结

📋 核心内容概述

本文探讨了人工智能在美国中学教育中的快速普及及其引发的争议。文章指出，尽管科技巨头和学区积极推动AI工具进入课堂，使用率已高达85%以上，但许多教师对此持保留态度。他们担心学生过度依赖AI会导致基础认知能力的退化，特别是写作和批判性思维能力的丧失。文章揭示了教育界面临的核心悖论：AI虽然能提高效率、减轻教师行政负担，但可能削弱教育本应培养的核心技能。一些教师开始抵制，提出"无AI课程"的概念，强调保留独立思考空间的重要性。最终，这场辩论关乎教育的本质目的——是追求效率，还是培养能够独立应对未来的思考者。

长难句解析

"By the end of last year, I realized my students couldn't do an assignment without AI now. They would fail if I told them to do it without AI."
【结构分析】时间状语 + 主句 + 条件状语从句
到去年年底，我意识到我的学生现在离开AI就无法完成作业了。如果我要求他们不用AI来做，他们就会不及格。
"The paradox of AI in education is that while it promises to personalize learning and free teachers from administrative drudgery, it also risks undermining the very skills education aims to develop."
【结构分析】主语 + 系动词 + 表语从句（while引导让步状语从句）
AI在教育中的悖论在于：虽然它承诺实现个性化学习并将教师从行政苦差中解放出来，但它也可能削弱教育旨在培养的核心技能。
"If students graduate unable to write coherent essays or articulate complex ideas without AI assistance, what does this mean for higher education and the workforce?"
【结构分析】If条件句 + 主句（what疑问句）
如果学生毕业时无法在没有AI帮助的情况下写出连贯的文章或清晰表达复杂的想法，这对高等教育和劳动力市场意味着什么？

阅读理解练习

1. What is the main concern of teachers regarding AI use in education?
[A] AI technology is too expensive for most schools.
[B] Students may lose the ability to think and write independently.
[C] AI systems are not accurate enough for education.
[D] Teachers will be replaced by AI assistants.
正确答案：B
解析：文章多处提到教师担心学生过度依赖AI会导致基础认知能力的退化。例如Peter Sedlack老师说"I think some of them, within the year, had lost the ability to actually do the work"，以及Maria Santos老师指出"Students are using AI to write essays, but they're not learning how to write"。
2. What does the phrase "draw a blank" in Paragraph 6 most probably mean?
[A] To refuse to answer questions
[B] To become confused and unable to respond
[C] To draw a picture on a blank paper
[D] To express ideas clearly
正确答案：B
解析：根据上下文，学生依赖AI写作，但当被要求解释自己的推理时，他们无法回答。"draw a blank"在此处意为"大脑一片空白，无法回答"，与选项B意思最接近。
3. What is the author's attitude toward the use of AI in education?
[A] Strongly supportive of integrating AI
[B] Completely opposed to any use of AI
[C] Objective but concerned about potential negative consequences
[D] Indifferent to whether AI is used
正确答案：C
解析：作者在文中既提到了AI在教育中的优势，也详细阐述了教师的担忧和潜在风险。最后一段作者提出"we may need to be more circumspect about how we deploy this technology"，表明作者持客观但谨慎担忧的态度。

阅读技巧提示

💡 考研英语阅读技巧

  • 主旨大意题：关注文章首尾段及各段首句，注意转折词（but, however）后的内容往往是作者真正想表达的观点。
  • 词义猜测题：利用上下文语境推断词义，特别注意同义替换和解释说明。
  • 推理判断题：注意区分事实陈述和观点态度，关注情态动词（may, might, need to）所传递的语气。
  • 长难句突破：先找主干（主谓宾/主系表），再分析从句和修饰成分。
  • 学术词汇积累：建议积累相关词汇如：personalized learning, cognitive skills, critical thinking, curriculum等。
