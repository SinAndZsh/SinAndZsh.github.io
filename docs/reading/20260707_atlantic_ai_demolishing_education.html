I'm a High Schooler. AI Is Demolishing My Education

人工智能正在摧毁我的教育——课堂批判性思维的终结

📖 正文精读

AI has transformed my experience of education. I am a senior at a public high school in New York, and these tools are everywhere. I do not want to use them in the way I see other kids my age using them — I generally choose not to — but they are inescapableinescapable /ˌɪnɪˈskeɪpəbl/ adj. 不可避免的；无法逃避的.

These annotations are used for discussions; we turn them in to our teacher at the end of class, and many of them are graded as part of our class participation. In Algebra II, after homework worksheets were passed around, I witnessed a peer use their phone to take a quick snapshot, which they then uploaded to ChatGPT. The AI quickly painted my classmate's screen with what it asserted to be a step-by-step solution and relevant graphs.

Many homework assignments are due by 11:59 p.m., to be submitted online via Google Classroom. We used to share memes about pounding away at the keyboard at 11:57, anxiously rushing to complete our work on time. These moments were not fun, exactly, but they did draw students together in a shared academic experience. Many of us were propelled by a kind of frantic productivity as we approached midnight, putting the finishing touches on our ideas and work. Now the deadline has been sapped of all meaning. AI has softened the consequencesconsequence /ˈkɑːnsɪkwens/ n. 后果；结果 of procrastinationprocrastination /prəˌkræstɪˈneɪʃn/ n. 拖延；耽搁 and led many students to avoid doing any work at all. As a consequence, these programs have destroyed much of what tied us together as students. There is little intensity anymore. Relatively few students seem to feel that the work is urgent or that they need to sharpen their own mind. We are struggling to receive the lessons of discipline that used to come from having to complete complicated work on a tight deadline, because chatbots promise to complete our tasks in seconds.

Desperate to address AI, schools across the U.S. are investing in detection tools and screen-monitoring software to curbcurb /kɜːrb/ v. 抑制；控制；约束 cheating. Some of these tools have been used in my school: Teachers rely on plagiarism checkers and exam-proctoring software. During in-class exams, screens may be locked or recording technology may be employed, but students have ways around these, too — sneaking phones in, for example. Based on what I've observed, preventative measures can only go so far.

The trouble with chatbots is not just that they allow students to get away withget away with 做（坏事）而未受惩罚 cheating or that they remove a sense of urgency from academics. The technology has also led students to focus on external results at the expense ofat the expense of 以……为代价 internal growth. The dominant worldview seems to be: Why worry about actually learning anything when you can get an A for outsourcing your thinking to a machine?

During my sophomore year, I participated in my school's debate team. I was excited to have a space outside the classroom where creativity, critical thinking, and intellectualintellectual /ˌɪntəˈlektʃuəl/ adj. 智力的；知识的 rigorrigor /ˈrɪɡər/ n. 严谨；严格 were valued and sharpened. I love the rush of building arguments from scratch. ChatGPT was released back in 2022, when I was a freshman, but the debate team weathered that first year without being overly influenced by the technology — at least as far as I could tell. But soon, AI took holdtake hold 扎根；站稳脚跟；确立 there as well. Many students avoided the technology and still stand againststand against 反对；抵抗 it, but it was impossible to ignore what we saw at competitions: chatbots being used for research and to construct arguments between rounds.

To me, debate is about forming your own arguments and pushing yourself to refuterefute /rɪˈfjuːt/ v. 驳斥；反驳 curveballcurveball /ˈkɜːrvbɔːl/ n. 曲线球；突如其来的难题 counters. It's about developing the skills to outthink and out-argue your opponent. It isn't about who can present the best cookie-cuttercookie-cutter /ˈkʊki ˈkʌtər/ adj. 千篇一律的；用模具切割的 AI arguments with polished and possibly invented data. Something I once loved now feels empty.

AI is not all bad. Some students may use these tools to develop their understanding or explore topics more deeply, serving their intellectual curiosity without actually cheating. AI can also be used as a study aid — say, quizzing you on vocabulary ahead of a Spanish test. But the temptation to abuse these tools is always there. I am concerned about what will happen as the short-term solutions presented by chatbots become the only ones that people know how to pursue — especially beyond the classroom. If we keep leaning onlean on 依赖；依靠 AI to sidestep pressure or deadlines, what happens when the tools aren't there? In the real world, chatbots cannot hold the powerful to accounthold...to account 使……承担责任 in the way an investigative reporter does, through relentless interviews and vettingvet /vet/ v. 审查；仔细检查 hard-to-find information. They cannot perform open-heart surgery or ballet. Many of us are so accustomed to outsourcing that we're dulling the very instincts that we need to prevailprevail /prɪˈveɪl/ v. 获胜；盛行；占优势 in life: grit, critical thinking, and the ability to function smoothly under stress.

It will take more than AI detectors and screen monitoring to address this disconnect. Student assessments should be focused on tasks that are not easily delegateddelegate /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ v. 委托；授权；委派 to technology: oral exams, for instance, in which students walk educators through their thinking process, or personalized writing assignments that are unique to the student or current events. Portfolio-basedportfolio /pɔːrtˈfoʊlioʊ/ n. 作品集；投资组合 or presentational grading could be emphasized over traditional exams or pop quizzes, giving students ample time to earn their grades. Students can also be encouraged to reflect on their own work — using learning journals or discussion to express their struggles, approaches, and lessons learned after each assignment.

These strategies could create an academic environment where integrityintegrity /ɪnˈteɡrəti/ n. 诚信；正直；完整, creativity, and original thought thrive. Whatever the path forward, it must be forged soon. If chatbots have made school easier to get through, they are also making school equally as hard to grow out of. The technology is producing a generation of eternal novicesnovice /ˈnɑːvɪs/ n. 新手；初学者, unable to think or perform for themselves.

📝 文章大意

主题：一名纽约公立高中高年级学生从自身视角出发，指出人工智能工具正在渗透并削弱美国中学的课堂学习，使作业、讨论和考试逐渐沦为“外包思维”的流水线。

主要内容：作者以弗雷德里克·道格拉斯回忆录阅读和代数作业为例，描述同学如何用ChatGPT生成批注、拍照搜题；她发现这类捷径已被严重“常态化”，Deadline 失去约束力，学生之间的共同学习体验也被瓦解。学校虽引入AI检测和屏幕监控，但学生通过“ humanizer”工具、手动改写、偷带手机等方式轻易绕过。作者还以辩论队经历说明，AI正在侵蚀本应培养批判性思维和独立论证的课外活动。

核心观点：AI并非一无是处，但其滥用正在造就“永恒的初学者”——一代缺乏毅力、批判性思维和抗压能力的学生。作者呼吁学校尽快改革评估方式：增加口头答辩、个性化写作、作品集评价等难以被技术代劳的任务，重建以诚信、创造力和原创思维为核心的学术环境。

考研英语阅读技巧

  • 主旨大意题：本文是一篇第一人称叙事夹议的文章，主旨往往在结尾段升华。注意末段“a generation of eternal novices”等关键句，可帮助锁定作者核心立场。
