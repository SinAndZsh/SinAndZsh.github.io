Washington Park, a four-story building with a red brick facade in downtown Newark, serves about 900 high school students. It is part of Uncommon Schools, a charter school network in the Northeast focused on college and career preparation.

He also developed firm guidelines for students on when to use — and when not to use — AI bots.

On a recent Tuesday, Mr Kern taught an Advanced Placement US History class on the Chicago Race Riot, violent protests set off by the murder of a Black teenager in 1919.

First, he asked students to read century-old newspaper clippings and other historical documents. Then, he led a class discussion on broader trends that had helped fuel the tensions.

Next, Mr Kern asked students to spend a few minutes describing the main cause of the riot to a chatbot he had created for the class. Allyson Johnson, 17, opened her laptop and typed in her answer: entrenched racial segregation.

"Let me push you on this," the chatbot responded. Segregation had existed for decades before 1919. So what specific factors, the bot asked, had caused a tense situation to suddenly escalate "into explosive violence"?

Ms Johnson said she enjoyed sparring with the AI because "the chatbot asked me different questions that pushed my argument even more."

After a few minutes, Mr Kern told students that their time with the chatbot was up and resumed the class discussion. Fundamentalfundamental /ˌfʌndəˈmentl/ adj. 根本的，基础的 student learning should remain an AI-free activity, he said.

📝 文章大意

主题：本文报道了美国新泽西州纽瓦克市华盛顿公园高中开设AI素养选修课的现象，探讨了学校如何在AI时代培养学生的批判性思维与负责任地使用技术的能力。

主要内容：随着生成式AI快速普及，美国各地学校正 hurry 推出AI素养课程，旨在帮助学生理解AI工具的工作原理、合理使用场景及潜在风险。文章以华盛顿公园高中为例，介绍了两位教师如何将AI融入历史课和职业探索课——他们开发定制化课堂聊天机器人，引导学生主动提问而非被动接受答案。同时，文章也呈现了教育研究者对AI可能削弱学生独立思考能力的担忧，以及学校如何在"全面禁止"与"放任使用"之间寻找平衡点的努力。

核心观点：AI素养教育不应将AI视为简单的答案生成器，而应培养学生"驾驭"技术的能力——知道何时使用AI辅助、何时坚持独立思考，最终让技术服务于人的成长而非取代人的思考。

🔍 长难句解析

"Teachers say they want to prepare young people to navigate a world increasingly shaped by AI, as chatbots manufacture human-sounding writing and employers use algorithms to help vet job candidates."
主干Teachers say + 宾语从句

从句分析
• 宾语从句：they want to prepare young people to navigate a world...
• 过去分词短语作后置定语：increasingly shaped by AI（修饰 world）
• as 引导原因状语从句，包含两个并列分句：
  ① chatbots manufacture human-sounding writing
  ② employers use algorithms to help vet job candidates

翻译教师们表示，他们希望帮助年轻人驾驭一个正日益被人工智能塑造的世界——因为聊天机器人正在制造听起来像人写的文字，而雇主们也在使用算法来筛选求职者。
"A recent study from Cambridge University Press Assessment and Microsoft Research found that students who took notes on text passages had better reading comprehension than students who got help from chatbots."
主干A recent study found that...

从句分析
• that 引导宾语从句：students... had better reading comprehension than students...
• 第一个 who 引导定语从句：who took notes on text passages（修饰第一个 students）
• 第二个 who 引导定语从句：who got help from chatbots（修饰第二个 students）

翻译剑桥大学出版社评估部门与微软研究部门近期的一项研究发现，对文本段落做笔记的学生比借助聊天机器人的学生拥有更好的阅读理解能力。
"Anytime where we want kids interacting with each other or doing initial critical thinking, I would never want AI or any sort of technology of that ilk to come in and interfere with that."
主干I would never want AI... to come in and interfere with that.

结构分析
• Anytime where... 为时间状语从句（where 引导定语从句修饰 anytime）
• 定语从句中包含两个并列的现在分词短语作宾补：
  ① interacting with each other
  ② doing initial critical thinking
• of that ilk：固定搭配，意为"那一类的"

翻译任何时候，只要我们希望孩子们相互交流或进行初步的批判性思考，我绝不希望人工智能或任何类似的技术介入并干扰这一过程。

💡 阅读技巧提示

考研英语阅读技巧

  • 主旨题定位法：英语一/二的主旨大意题通常可通过首段+各段首句快速把握。本文首段即点明"AI literacy class"这一核心话题。
  • 例证题注意：文中出现具体人名（如Mike Taubman, Scott Kern）、地名（Newark, Washington Park High）时，往往是为了支撑前文观点，而非独立考点。
  • 长难句拆解：遇到as, while, where等引导的从句时，先找主干（主谓宾），再分析修饰成分。本文多处使用as引导原因/时间状语从句。
  • 观点态度题：注意区分"作者观点"与"引用观点"。考研常考文中某人的态度，需准确定位引号内的直接引语。
  • 词汇题策略：生词含义可通过上下文逻辑（同义/反义/举例）推断，如"erode critical thinking"中，erode可通过"enable cheating"的并列负面含义推测为"削弱"。
