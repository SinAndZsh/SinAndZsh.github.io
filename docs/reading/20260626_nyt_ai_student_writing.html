How A.I. Killed Student Writing (and Revived It)

AI如何"杀死"学生写作，又如何让它"复活"

📅 2026年4月30日 ⏱ 阅读时长约 15 分钟 📖 词数：约 1800 词 🏆 难度：考研英语一

💡 文章大意

本文选自《纽约时报》2026年4月刊，由教育记者Dana Goldstein撰写，深入报道了人工智能（AI）对美国高中和大学写作教学的颠覆性影响。调查显示，62%的美国学生定期使用AI完成作业，传统"带回家写论文"的模式正迅速瓦解。近400位教育工作者的反馈揭示了一个深刻的教育变革：教师们纷纷要求学生在课堂内写作，甚至回归纸笔方式，以确保评估学生真实的写作能力。文章穿插了多个学校的具体案例，展现了教育界应对AI挑战的多元策略，同时探讨了AI技术素养与学生批判性思维培养之间的张力。

For today's high school and college students, the all-night writing session一段时间；会议；座谈会, hunched over a laptop at home or in a library carrel, is on the way out.

In the era of artificial intelligence, take-home writing assignments have become so difficult to police监督；管制；巡查 for integrity that many educators have simply stopped assigning them.

Instead, in a rapid迅速的；急剧的 shift, teachers are requiring students to write inside the classroom, where they can be observed. Assignments have changed too, with some educators prompting促使；提示；激励 students to reflect on反思；回顾 their personal reactions反应；回应 to what they've learned and read — the type of writing that A.I. struggles to credibly可信地；可靠地 produce.

This transformation转变；变革 is happening across the educational landscape全景；局面；形势, from suburban districts and urban charter schools to community colleges and the Ivy League.

The New York Times heard from nearly 400 college and high school educators who responded to a callout征集；呼吁 about how generative生成的；有生产力的 A.I. is changing writing instruction. Almost all described a deep rethinking of how to teach writing — and whether it still matters, since A.I. has become a better writer than most students (and adults), they said.

Teachers are responding to a widespread challenge. Over the past year, A.I. use has become ubiquitous无处不在的；普遍存在的 among American students. Between May and December of 2025, the share of American middle school, high school and college students who reported regularly using A.I. for homework increased from 48 to 62 percent, according to polling民意调查 from RAND — even as two-thirds of students said the technology harmed critical-thinking批判性思维 skills. A third of the students reported using A.I. to draft or revise writing.

The Revival of Paper and Pencil

Over the past year, Jessica Binney, 49, overhauled彻底改造；全面修订 her English classroom at John Jay High School in the Katonah-Lewisboro school district, north of New York City. She gave up on assigning three-to-five-page papers, once a staple主要产品；支柱；常备元素 of the homework in her Advanced Placement courses. Now, her students write in-class essays, either by hand or on a laptop with a locked-down锁定的；受限制的 browser.

Ms. Binney regrets后悔；遗憾 the loss of depth that longer assignments could produce. But she and many other educators who have moved writing into the classroom described relief宽慰；解脱 at being able to abandon the highly imperfect不完美的；有缺陷的 science of A.I. detection. Student reliance依赖；信赖 on chatbots had gotten “worse and worse” as the technology gained sophistication精密；复杂；老练, Ms. Binney said.

One April afternoon in her A.P. literature class, Ms. Binney read aloud “XIV,” a poem by the St. Lucian Nobel laureate获奖者；桂冠诗人 Derek Walcott. It describes the poet and his brother as children, trekking跋涉；徒步旅行 into the Caribbean forest to listen at the feet of a traditional storyteller讲故事的人.

Walcott's language is lush and challenging. Students marked up paper handouts of the text, underlining在...下面划线；强调 and scrawling in the margins. Then they took out notebooks and began to draft essays analyzing literary devices.

“I want you to write out a really rough, terrible draft in your writers' notebooks,” Ms. Binney told them. “And then I want you to scratch out划掉；涂掉 and rewrite it.”

There was nary a laptop or tablet in sight. For these juniors and seniors, who have been taught on screens for much of their schooling, Ms. Binney's class can be a welcome break.

“It's a relief,” said Cassady Tondorf, 17. “There's less distraction分心；干扰.”

Her classmate Naomi Siegel, also 17, agreed. “I'm able to connect with people more easily throughout the class, because we're not as much on our computers.”

If there is a downside缺点；不利因素, it is that today's teenagers have little experience writing with pens and pencils. Their handwriting笔迹；手写 can be atrocious极差的；糟糕的. Still, many educators said they were willing to deal with that inconvenience不便；麻烦 in order to ensure they were grading authentic真实的；可信的 student work.

At Kingsborough Community College in Brooklyn, Matthew Gartner said that because of A.I. overuse过度使用, he now has his freshman composition作文；构成 students write on paper in the classroom for 30 minutes, then share their drafts immediately in small groups.

“It creates connection and a desire to communicate well,” he said.

Jane-Marie Law, a religious studies professor at Cornell, said she recently realized that despite asking her students to sign an honor code promising not to use A.I. for writing, they were still doing so.

“Gone were any errors,” she said. “Also gone was a sense of freshness and daring. ChatGPT made everything so safe.”

This fall, she plans to move toward mandatory, in-class writing by hand.

At the University of Virginia, Devin Donovan, who teaches writing and rhetoric修辞学；雄辩术, requires students to write on paper in class, and revise drafts using scissors and tape to cut up and reorder重新排序；重新安排 paragraphs.

At the end of the semester, a final piece is polished and submitted via computer — a common approach.

“I've moved past the idea of catching people or punishing惩罚 this,” Professor Donovan said of A.I. His new method fosters “a real person-to-person experience, which is sort of unfakeable无法伪造的；不可假冒的.”

Resisting Temptation

Most teachers and students are navigating A.I. without clear guidance from school administrators管理者；行政人员 or policymakers政策制定者. Leaders have denounced谴责；公开指责 cheating, but have also praised the technology's promise, often without offering many specifics on how students should and should not be using A.I.

Young people are using it. Recent studies of ChatGPT and of Anthropic's Claude chatbot show that help with schoolwork is among the most popular uses of generative A.I. When it comes to writing, two-thirds of ChatGPT queries ask for edits or translation翻译. A third ask the chatbot to generate生成；产生 text from scratch.

Megan Hart, an English teacher at South Forsyth High School outside of Atlanta, said that last year, she noticed take-home essays returned sounding eerily诡异地；令人毛骨悚然地 similar and formulaic公式化的；刻板的.

At the same time, her district has encouraged teachers to get comfortable with generative A.I., she said. Several of Dr. Hart's former students have told her they use A.I. frequently频繁地；经常地 in their adult jobs, helping to convince说服；使确信 her that teenagers need to develop A.I. skills.

Now, she requires students to complete most writing in the classroom, but she also teaches them how to use A.I. to find reliable可靠的；可信赖的 sources for research papers. And she has worked with students to use A.I. to solicit征求；请求 feedback on drafts.

“The kids have to build that critical thinking,” she said, including fact-checking事实核查 the information A.I. provides. “This is an assistant that is here to help me. But it can also really make me look like an idiot白痴；傻瓜.”

Breton Sheridan, who teaches English at a Philadelphia charter high school, has prioritized优先考虑；将...放在首位 in-classroom reading and writing, oral presentations展示；演讲 and debates.

“They are using generative A.I. to write before they learn how to write. They are reading ChatGPT summaries of a book before they have ever read a book,” he said. “The result is a diminished减弱的；缩小的 population人口；群体.”

But he noted that schools serving low-income students, like his, are often under the most pressure to show that they are embracing innovative创新的；革新的 technology and preparing students for the working world, where it may soon be standard to rely on generative A.I.

The shift toward in-classroom writing is part of a broader conversation about how educators can counteract对抗；抵消 the negative impact影响；冲击 of screen time on attention and learning. Laptops, tablets and gamified learning apps entered most classrooms over the past decade, but there is little evidence they increased student achievement成就；成绩.

Rather, test scores declined over the same period, especially in reading.

Still, the tech industry continues to aggressively激进地；强势地 market its products to schools. Some A.I. start-ups are appealing directly to students through funny, tongue-in-cheek半开玩笑的；调侃的 social media videos featuring attractive有吸引力的；迷人的 young influencers有影响力的人；网红. The videos coach students on how to use generative A.I. to breeze through轻松完成；迅速通过 writing-related coursework.

Educators said that while A.I. may be helpful for research and revision, they still want students to face down勇敢面对；直面 the blank page and craft original text.

Daniel Herman, who teaches humanities人文学科 at Maybeck High School, a private school in Berkeley, Calif., said he continues to see student writing as essential必要的；本质的, “to help them become better readers, thinkers and explorers of the world and their mind.”

Teenagers said they were eager for adults to guide them on how to use A.I. ethically合乎道德地；伦理地 and productively高效地；富有成效地.

“It's really concerning how dependent依赖的；从属的 we are,” said Ms. Siegel, the John Jay senior.

She hopes to become a defense attorney律师 and recently decided to reel back收敛；减少；回拉 her use of A.I. for editing because she found it introduced a stilted生硬的；不自然的 voice into her writing.

“But,” she added, “we're getting into a world full of technology, and we have to learn how to allow it to benefit us.”

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main purpose of this article?
