The Jobs Apocalypse: A Short History

为AI就业末日做好准备：历史告诉我们什么

📅 2026年5月14日 ⏱ 阅读时长约 12 分钟 📖 词数：约 1500 词 🏆 难度：考研英语一

💡 文章大意

本文选自《经济学人》2026年5月刊，探讨了人工智能是否会导致大规模失业这一全球热议话题。文章指出，尽管普通民众和AI企业领袖都对就业前景感到悲观，但经济学家们认为历史经验表明技术扩散总是缓慢的，从未出现过新技术快速蔓延导致大规模长期失业的情况。作者通过回顾农业革命、20世纪中叶的技术变革以及英国工业革命等历史案例，说明技术变革虽然会改变就业结构，但不会造成灾难性失业。文章最后指出，如果AI真的要引发"就业末日"，首先会表现为生产力飙升而实际工资停滞，而这种信号至今尚未出现。

At no time从不；绝不 in polling history have Americans been less optimistic乐观的；乐观主义的 about their long-term employment就业；雇用 prospects前景；前途. The average person believes they have a 22% chance of losing their job in the next five years, according to one survey, a higher share than even during the global financial crisis of 2007-09. The cause of this gloom忧郁；沮丧；阴暗 is artificial intelligence. Nearly one in five American workers recently told another pollster that AI or automation自动化 is "very" or "somewhat" likely to replace them.

It isn't just average people who are alarmed惊慌的；担忧的. So are the leaders of the very AI companies causing the anxiety. Dario Amodei of Anthropic has warned that AI could push unemployment to 10-20%. Bill Gates, co-founder of Microsoft, said that in an AI world people will not be needed for "most things". Sam Altman, boss of OpenAI, has clocked that talking up the technology's disruptive破坏性的；颠覆性的 power is provoking a backlash, and now speaks of "tools to augment增强；扩充 and elevate people, not entities实体；存在 to replace them". But even he could not resist mentioning "disruption/significant transition过渡；转变 as we switch to new jobs".

Economists经济学家 are, for a change, far less dismal阴沉的；惨淡的；差劲的. They are allergic to the "lump of labour fallacy劳动总量谬误（认为工作岗位数量固定不变的经济学谬误）" which treats the jobs market as static静态的；停滞的 and zero-sum零和的. If technology displaces取代；替代；转移 workers from some occupations职业；工作, they argue, it enriches使富裕；使丰富 others, who then spend their gains on goods and services that create new employment.

长难句解析 ①
"If technology displaces workers from some occupations, they argue, it enriches others, who then spend their gains on goods and services that create new employment."
结构分析：这是一个复合句。If引导条件状语从句；they argue为插入语；主句为it enriches others；who引导非限制性定语从句修饰others；that引导限制性定语从句修饰goods and services。
翻译：他们认为，如果技术将工人从某些职业中取代，它也会使其他人富裕起来，这些人随后会将收益花在创造新就业的商品和服务上。

The labour market certainly is not cracking yet. The share of the OECD's working-age population with a job keeps breaking records, unemployment across the club of mostly rich countries is just 5%, and America employs more people than ever in "AI-exposed" industries like law. American graduates have been struggling since before OpenAI launched ChatGPT in late 2022. Many economists foresee relatively little disruption ahead. Those at America's Bureau of Labour Statistics think the country will add 5.2m jobs between 2024 and 2034, increasing total employment by 3%.

Advances in AI's capabilities could render使成为；使变得 current data, and extrapolations外推；推断 from this, obsolete过时的；淘汰的. But if this happened, and AI really were to put millions of people out of work, it would be unprecedented前所未有的；史无前例的 in human history. Never have new technologies spread fast enough to make large numbers of people unemployed for a long time. Understanding why may shed light on阐明；使...清楚明白 how this time is—and is not—different.

Historical Lessons

Historical data suggest that technological diffusion扩散；传播 always proceeds slowly. In a paper published in 2012 Robert Gordon of Northwestern University found that since 1300, growth of GDP per person at whatever was the world's most sophisticated复杂的；精致的；老练的 economy of its time has never exceeded about 2.5% a year. When other countries grew faster than this, they did so by catching up with赶上；追上 a richer place that, almost by definition, sparked earlier wealth-creating technological progress. And the fact that growth at the frontier前沿；边境 of innovation创新；革新 was slower meant that so was the pace of any job destruction.

长难句解析 ②
"The fact that growth at the frontier of innovation was slower meant that so was the pace of any job destruction."
结构分析：The fact that...为主语从句，that引导同位语从句解释fact的内容；meant为谓语；that so was...为宾语从句，其中so was为倒装结构，代替the pace of job destruction was slower。
翻译：创新前沿的增长较慢这一事实意味着，任何就业破坏的速度也较慢。

Take farming. Although it has undergone经历；经受 monumental巨大的；重要的 technological upheavals剧变；动乱 over the past millennium, farm employment has changed only slowly. The share of England's labour force劳动力 in agriculture has been falling steadily稳定地；持续地 since the 16th century without ever collapsing崩溃；倒塌 suddenly. The recognisably modern tractor was invented in America at the start of the 20th century, and it took generations代；世代 rather than years for the agricultural workforce劳动力；职工总数 to decline.

Even when job disruption is faster, workers need not suffer. In the middle of the 20th century the first computers, shipping containers集装箱 and other wonders led Harold Wilson, a British prime minister, to describe the "white heat of technology" burning through Western economies. GDP per person in America, which had by then dislodged取代；移开 Britain as the world's frontier economy, grew by 2.5% a year, the fastest ever for a leading economic power. The level of job disruption, as measured by the share of employment shifting between industries or occupations, was at times more than twice as high as it is today. Yet many people look back fondly on that era as a time of rising wages, widening opportunity and unpolarised非极化的；未分化的 politics.

The Industrial Revolution

One instance of technological change has become notorious臭名昭著的；声名狼藉的: the Industrial Revolution in 19th-century Britain. According to some accounts, it was horribly disruptive to workers. James Watt's inventions in the 1760-1780s made steam engines efficient enough to power factories. This led to a period of blistering极快的；猛烈的 economic growth that appeared to coincide with与...同时发生 stagnation停滞；滞胀 in inflation-adjusted经通胀调整的 wages. Between 1790 and 1840 these barely budged移动；改变, even as capitalists earned vast巨大的；广阔的 profits.

长难句解析 ③
"This led to a period of blistering economic growth that appeared to coincide with stagnation in inflation-adjusted wages."
结构分析：主句为This led to a period of blistering economic growth；that引导限制性定语从句修饰growth；appear to为系表结构；coincide with stagnation为不定式短语；in inflation-adjusted wages为介词短语修饰stagnation。
翻译：这导致了一段迅猛的经济增长期，而这似乎与经通胀调整后的工资停滞同时发生。

Today's "thought leaders" in Silicon Valley often invoke援引；祈求 this pause. It is associated with Friedrich Engels, a capitalist-heir-turned-communist who described it in "The Condition of the Working Class in England", his account of Manchester's slums in the 1840s. Recent scholarship, though, casts doubt on对...产生怀疑 whether "Engels' pause" is a useful blueprint蓝图；行动计划 for what AI may have in store for workers.

The composition构成；成分 of British employment saw little churn流动；更替 until the 1850s, and then only as much as it does today. Moreover, if technology destroyed jobs, it created plenty more. Between 1760 and 1860 the number of Britons in work ballooned激增；膨胀 from 4.5m to 12m. Unemployment generally remained modest适度的；谦虚的. Wage growth was indeed slow during Engels' pause—but no slower than in the half-century before it. This reflected slow productivity生产力；生产效率 growth in the Industrial Revolution's early years, itself a function of the gradual diffusion of Watt's technological breakthroughs.

By 1830 only about 160,000 horsepower was in use in the whole of Britain, equivalent to 1,000 typical modern cars. Given rapid population growth in the period, it is a "truly remarkable achievement" that workers' purchasing power购买力 grew at all, as Sir Tony Wrigley, a late British demographer人口统计学家, put it. It looks even more remarkable if you adjust wages not by the consumer-price index, as historians tend to, but by the average price of domestically国内地；本地地 produced output, the "GDP deflator紧缩指数；平减指数".

长难句解析 ④
"The average employer paid workers reasonably fairly after selling his wares and deducting the cost of materials."
结构分析：主句为The average employer paid workers reasonably fairly；after selling his wares and deducting the cost of materials为现在分词短语作时间状语，其中selling和deducting为并列结构。
翻译：普通雇主在出售商品并扣除原材料成本后，向工人支付的报酬是相当公平的。

The gap between the two measures of real wages illustrates a crucial point about the Industrial Revolution. The average employer paid workers reasonably fairly after selling his wares and deducting扣除；减去 the cost of materials. He did not profit from exploiting剥削；利用 his staff, as Engels supposed. The problem for labourers was less unfair pay than sharp rises in the cost of living. Food prices rose steadily, and sometimes soared, because of war and high tariffs关税 on grain imports. The villains反派；罪魁祸首 of the Industrial Revolution were politicians, not machines.

This puts a different gloss on the industrial unrest动荡；不安 of the period. In the early 19th century textile workers revolted反抗；反叛, destroying the power looms织布机 they thought would kill their craft. A few years later farm labourers smashed threshing脱粒 machines across southern England. Historians link such unrest to technological disruption, yet strikes and wrecking破坏；拆毁 are as old as time. In England, riots were less frequent in the early 1800s—the middle of Engels' pause— than later in the century, when real wages were growing strongly. The Chartists, who secured suffrage选举权；投票权 and other rights for working men, did not gain ground until wage growth was unpaused in the 1840s.

Nicholas Crafts, an economic historian, summed it up总结；概括 neatly. The Industrial Revolution, he wrote, is "not a template模板；样板" for "technological change that boosts productivity at the expense of以...为代价 a significant …decline in labour's share of national income". In short, those warning of AI-driven mass unemployment are predicting something that has never happened before. That does not mean it can never happen at all. The first signs would be sharply rising productivity combined with weak real-wage growth in America, the world's frontier economy. This would show up as表现为；显现为 an increase in GDP per person, above Mr Gordon's ceiling of 2.5%, and a simultaneous同时的 jump in corporate profits, reflecting the gains from higher output flowing to capital, not labour. Another signal would be big job losses in lots of industries.

长难句解析 ⑤
"This would show up as an increase in GDP per person, above Mr Gordon's ceiling of 2.5%, and a simultaneous jump in corporate profits, reflecting the gains from higher output flowing to capital, not labour."
结构分析：主句为This would show up as an increase...and a simultaneous jump...；above Mr Gordon's ceiling of 2.5%为插入语修饰increase；reflecting the gains...为现在分词短语作伴随状语；from higher output为介词短语修饰gains；flowing to capital, not labour为现在分词短语修饰output。
翻译：这将表现为人均GDP的增长（超过戈登先生设定的2.5%上限），以及企业利润的同步跃升，反映出更高产出带来的收益流向了资本而非劳动。

History holds a final lesson. If disruption is coming, it will show up in a recession衰退；不景气. Downturns cleanse净化；清除 the economy of unproductive低效的；无生产力的 jobs. Companies must make radical根本的；激进的 changes to survive; weak firms go under; capital and labour moves to more productive ones. Almost all of America's once-routine常规的；例行的 jobs have vanished消失；消亡 during past downturns. Which ones vanish next time will offer a big hint暗示；线索. Until then everyone—including Messrs Amodei, Gates and Altman—will remain none the wiser更明智的；更聪明的 about the shape of the AI world to come.

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of this article?
A. AI will definitely cause mass unemployment in the next five years.
B. Historical evidence suggests that technological change rarely leads to mass unemployment, despite current fears about AI.
C. The Industrial Revolution was the most disruptive event in human history.
D. Economists disagree with AI company leaders on everything.
正确答案：B
文章开篇提出人们对AI导致失业的担忧，但随后通过回顾农业革命、20世纪技术变革和工业革命等历史案例，论证了技术变革虽然会改变就业结构，但从未导致大规模长期失业。经济学家认为历史数据表明技术扩散总是缓慢的，"就业末日"的预言缺乏历史依据。A选项与文章观点相反；C选项过于片面，文章只是将工业革命作为案例之一；D选项过于绝对，文中并未说 economists disagree on everything。
2. 【细节理解题】According to the article, what does recent scholarship suggest about "Engels' pause"?
A. It proves that the Industrial Revolution was extremely harmful to workers.
B. It shows that technological change always destroys jobs without creating new ones.
C. It casts doubt on whether it is a useful blueprint for AI's impact on workers.
D. It demonstrates that real wages fell dramatically during the Industrial Revolution.
正确答案：C
文章第七段明确提到"Recent scholarship, though, casts doubt on whether 'Engels' pause' is a useful blueprint for what AI may have in store for workers"（然而，近期学术研究质疑"恩格斯停顿"是否是预测AI对工人影响的有用参照）。A选项与文中"employers did not profit from exploiting their staff"相悖；B选项与"if technology destroyed jobs, it created plenty more"矛盾；D选项与"wage growth was indeed slow...but no slower than in the half-century before it"不符。
3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred from the last paragraph about the author's view on AI's future impact?
A. The author is certain that AI will not cause any job losses.
B. The author believes that recession is the only way to identify which jobs AI will eliminate.
C. The author suggests that no one, including AI experts, can predict the exact shape of AI's impact on employment at present.
D. The author argues that AI will definitely trigger a recession in the near future.
正确答案：C
文章最后一句"Until then everyone—including Messrs Amodei, Gates and Altman—will remain none the wiser about the shape of the AI world to come"明确指出，在那之前（即经济衰退发生前），所有人——包括AI领域的顶级专家——都无法确切预知AI世界的形态。A选项过于绝对，文中说"That does not mean it can never happen at all"；B选项曲解文意，文中说衰退会提供线索（offer a big hint），而非"唯一方式"；D选项将条件关系误读为确定性结论，文中说的是"If disruption is coming, it will show up in a recession"。
