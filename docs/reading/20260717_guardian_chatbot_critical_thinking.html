Chatbot Dependence Can Erode Critical Thinking

Relying on chatbots to sort fact from fiction may make that job easier in the moment, but it can also leave people less able to do it on their own over time.

✍️ MIT Media Lab / The Guardian 📅 原文：2026-07-17 / 精读：2026-07-17 📖 约 1,350 词 🎯 考研英语一难度

📋 文章大意

本文选自《卫报》对麻省理工学院（MIT）一项最新研究的报道。研究指出，虽然聊天机器人能在短期内帮助人们识别虚假新闻和篡改图片，但长期过度依赖可能导致独立批判性思维能力下降。文章介绍了实验设计、核心数据与深层担忧，探讨了“即时准确”与“长期独立判断”之间的权衡，并进一步延伸至教育、医疗和公共媒体素养领域，呼吁将 AI 设计为“强化判断”而非“替代判断”的工具。

A 2025 study published in The Lancet found that physicians who used AI tools for cancer detection eventually became less effective when asked to identify cases independently. Elsewhere, experts have warned that constant cognitive offloading to AI may weaken mental discipline and reduce the effort required for sustained attention and memory. The basic idea is simple: skills that are not used frequently enough tend to fade. If a chatbot handles the verification work every time, users may lose the habit of asking the questions that build durable judgment.

The MIT researchers say the findings should matter a great deal to educators, who are increasingly adopting AI-powered tools in classrooms and homework systems. Those tools can be valuable, especially when they support feedback, accessibility and personalized learning. Still, there is a risk that convenience will crowd out deep learning. That concern extends beyond schools. Workplace training, professional certification and public information campaigns increasingly include AI-generated guidance. If those systems are designed poorly, they may create users who are efficient but fragile: able to act when the assistant is present, but less capable of independent judgment when it is not. Like any early research, the MIT experiment has boundaries that should shape how it is interpreted. The sample size was modest, and most participants came from the United States and the United Kingdom. The study also lasted only four weeks, which is long enough to observe a shift but not long enough to determine whether the decline in independent judgment levels off, deepens or reverses with continued exposure.

The implications of the study stretch into daily life. Misinformation is no longer confined to obscure corners of the web. It appears in political discourse, health advice, consumer scams and viral social posts. At the same time, AI tools are increasingly embedded in search engines, productivity platforms and messaging apps. That means the average person is more likely than ever to encounter a chatbot while trying to figure out whether something is true. If those systems help people think critically, they can be part of the solution. If they quietly replace critical thinking, they can deepen the problem.

The study does not argue for abandoning chatbots. Instead, it points toward a more deliberate relationship with them. AI can be useful when it helps users inspect evidence, compare claims and sharpen their own reasoning. It becomes riskier when it provides certainty too quickly or encourages passive acceptance. The researchers' core message is that AI should be built to support judgment, not replace it. For users, that means treating chatbot answers as a starting point rather than a final verdict. For developers, it means designing systems that prompt verification, surface uncertainty and encourage people to check the underlying evidence. For educators and employers, it means making sure AI assistance does not eliminate the practice required to build durable skill.

🔍 长难句解析

Relying on chatbots to sort fact from fiction may make that job easier in the moment, but it can also leave people less able to do it on their own over time, according to new research from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
【结构分析】主语为动名词短语"Relying on chatbots to sort fact from fiction"；谓语为"may make... but... can also leave..."的并列结构；"according to..."为介词短语作状语，说明信息来源。【句意】麻省理工学院的一项新研究表明，依赖聊天机器人来辨别真伪或许能让当下这项工作变得更轻松，但也可能使人们随着时间推移越来越无法独立完成这项任务。
The deeper question is whether that assistance changes how people learn, remember and reason when the tool is absent.
【结构分析】主句为"The deeper question is whether..."，其中 whether 引导表语从句；表语从句中又包含 how 引导的宾语从句"how people learn, remember and reason"，以及 when 引导的时间状语从句"when the tool is absent"。【句意】更深层的问题是：这种辅助是否会改变人们在缺少该工具时学习、记忆和推理的方式。
A chatbot that delivers a fast, polished explanation can create the impression that the user is becoming smarter or more capable, when in reality the user may simply be outsourcing the hardest part of the work.
【结构分析】主语为"A chatbot"，后接 that 引导的定语从句"that delivers a fast, polished explanation"；谓语为"can create"，宾语为"the impression"，后接 that 引导的同位语从句解释 impression 的内容；"when in reality..."为状语从句，表示对比。【句意】一个能快速给出润色完善解释的聊天机器人会制造一种用户变得更聪明、更有能力的错觉，而现实中用户可能只是把工作中最困难的部分外包了出去。

✅ 阅读理解练习

1. 【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of the passage?
  1. MIT researchers have proved that chatbots are entirely harmful to human cognition.
  2. AI chatbots can help detect misinformation immediately but may weaken independent critical thinking over time.
  3. The internet has become too saturated with misinformation for ordinary users to handle.
  4. AI tools should be banned from classrooms to protect students' foundational skills.
正确答案：B
解析：全文围绕 MIT 研究展开，核心论点是聊天机器人短期内提升虚假信息识别准确率，但长期过度依赖会削弱独立批判性思维。A 项“完全有害”过于绝对，与文中“AI can be useful”不符；C 项只涉及背景信息；D 项“应禁止 AI”与文章“not argue for abandoning chatbots”相悖。
2. 【细节理解题】According to the MIT study, what happened to participants' unaided performance by the fourth week?
  1. It improved by 21%.
  2. It remained basically unchanged.
  3. It declined by 15.3%.
  4. It became better than their AI-assisted performance.
正确答案：C
解析：文章第四段明确指出，"Their unaided performance in spotting misinformation declined by 15.3% by the fourth week of the experiment"。A 项 21% 是有 AI 辅助时的提升幅度；B、D 两项与原文相反。
3. 【推理判断题】What can be inferred about the design of AI tools from the passage?
  1. The fastest answer is always the best answer.
  2. AI should be designed to strengthen users' reasoning rather than replace it.
  3. Users prefer slow and reflective interactions with chatbots.
  4. Only students and journalists need to worry about chatbot dependence.
正确答案：B
解析：文章第八段提出问题"should systems simply deliver the fastest answer, or should they be built to strengthen the user's own reasoning process?"，并在结尾强调"AI should be built to support judgment, not replace it"，因此 B 正确。A 与文意相反；C 将研究者观点误当作用户偏好；D 与文中"not only for students and journalists, but for anyone"矛盾。

💡 阅读技巧提示

  • 识别“问题—实验—发现—启示”结构：本文先提出核心矛盾（AI 即时帮助 vs 长期能力退化），再介绍实验、呈现数据、分析原因，最后延伸至教育等场景。把握该结构有助于快速定位各题答案。
  • 警惕绝对化选项：考研阅读正确选项通常避免“entirely”“always”“only”等绝对词汇。本文主张平衡使用 AI，而非彻底否定或完全肯定。
  • 关注转折与对比：but, yet, by contrast, in reality 等信号词常引出作者真正强调的观点，也是命题高频点。
  • 数据定位细节：文中出现 21%、15.3%、67、four weeks 等具体数字，阅读时可快速标记，便于细节题和推理题作答。
  • 积累科技/教育类高频词：misinformation, credibility, cognition, foundational, outsource, amplify 等为考研英语一常见话题词汇，建议结合语境记忆。
