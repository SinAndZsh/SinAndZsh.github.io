阅读理解练习

1. 主旨大意题：What is the main argument of the passage?
  1. The Library of Alexandria was destroyed by war and its loss led directly to the Dark Ages.
  2. Americans are reading fewer books than ever, and this decline threatens the cognitive foundations of civilization.
  3. Graphic novels and young-adult fiction have replaced serious literature as the dominant reading material.
  4. The printing press was the single most important invention in human history after writing itself.
正确答案：B
答案解析：文章以亚历山大图书馆的衰落作为引子，但核心论点是当代美国阅读习惯的严重退化及其深远影响。作者用大量数据证明美国人阅读量骤降、阅读能力下滑，并在后半部分深入论述读写能力如何塑造人类文明，警示阅读衰落的文明级后果。A项是文中被反驳的观点（历史学家认为主要是疏忽而非战争）；C项仅为细节支撑；D项偏离文章主旨。
2. 细节理解题：According to the passage, what does Maryanne Wolf argue about Americans' reading ability?
  1. Americans have completely lost the ability to read and decode written words.
  2. Americans are reading more words than ever but losing higher-order comprehension and synthesis abilities.
  3. Americans' basic reading skills have improved due to increased exposure to digital text.
  4. Americans should return to oral traditions as a more natural form of communication.
正确答案：B
答案解析：根据文章第九段，UCLA神经科学家Maryanne Wolf认为"people are losing the ability to think deeply about writing. That doesn't mean they are forgetting how to decode individual words. Rather, they are losing the higher-order abilities of comprehension and synthesis"（人们正在丧失深入思考文本的能力。这并不意味着他们忘记了如何解码单个单词。相反，他们正在丧失高阶的理解与综合思维能力）。A项与原文"doesn't mean they are forgetting how to decode"直接矛盾；C项与文中"basic reading skills are declining"相悖；D项文中未提及。
3. 推理判断题：What can be inferred from the passage about the author's view on the future of reading?
  1. The author is optimistic that technology will eventually reverse the decline in reading.
  2. The author believes the decline of reading is irreversible but its consequences are overstated.
  3. The author warns that the postliterate trend poses fundamental threats to democratic society and scientific progress.
  4. The author argues that oral communication is superior to written communication in the digital age.
正确答案：C
答案解析：作者在文末明确提出"As we enter a postliterate age, we risk losing the very habits of mind that made democratic self-government and scientific progress possible"（当我们进入后读写时代，我们有可能丧失那些使民主自治和科学进步成为可能的心智习惯），并追问"whether we even recognize what is at stake"（我们是否认识到什么正处于危险之中）。这表明作者认为后读写趋势对民主社会和科学进步构成根本性威胁。A项"optimistic"与全文基调相反；B项"consequences are overstated"与作者反复强调的严重性矛盾；D项文中明确肯定读写对文明的塑造作用，未主张口语优于书面语。
