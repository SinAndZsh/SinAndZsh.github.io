Big Tech Is Finally Under the Microscope

📖 正文精读

Editorial: Scrutiny of the impact of technology on children's lives and education should be welcomed.

A new law新法律 banning mobile phone use in schools in England, which ministers reluctantly/rɪˈlʌktəntli/ 不情愿地，勉强地 agreed to last week, is on one level the result of political manoeuvring/məˈnuːvərɪŋ/ 策略操作，操纵 by Liberal Democrat and Conservative peers/pɪəz/ 贵族，同辈 — who forced their hand by threatening to derail/dɪˈreɪl/ 使脱轨，破坏 the schools bill. Until now, the government's position has been that advice to headteachers/ˈhedtiːtʃəz/ 中小学校长 was sufficient. But whether or not a ban turns out to be helpful, the campaign/kæmˈpeɪn/ 运动，活动 reflects deepening/ˈdiːpənɪŋ/ 加深的，日益严重的 public concern about the degree to which powerful tech companies can be trusted/ˈtrʌstɪd/ 信任，信赖.

From messaging platforms where pupils and teachers interact, to appointment-booking/əˈpɔɪntmənt ˈbʊkɪŋ/ 预约 systems and research carried out in lessons and at home, digital technology is deeply embedded/ɪmˈbedɪd/ 嵌入的，深植的 in education. This should not be expected to change. Classrooms rightly reflect the wider world that they are part of. But the current push towards stronger scrutiny/ˈskruːtəni/ 审查，监督 of screens in schools — and in young people's lives more broadly — is justified by accruing/əˈkruːɪŋ/ 积累的，逐渐增加 evidence about their impacts.

In Norway and Sweden, policies promoting the use of iPads and laptops have been reversed/rɪˈvɜːst/ 撤销，反转 in favour of books and handwriting/ˈhændraɪtɪŋ/ 手写 after both countries recorded drops in reading scores. In the UK, new guidance recommends that under-fives should spend no more than one hour on screens each day, and should not watch fast-paced, social-media style videos at all. Even in California, home of the US tech industry, the mood is shifting: from September, elementary and middle-school students in Los Angeles will face restrictions/rɪˈstrɪkʃənz/ 限制，约束 on device use.

"Whether legal age limits, advice, or a focus on what happens in classrooms is the best response is highly contested."
【结构分析】主语为Whether引导的主语从句（Whether...response），谓语是is，表语是highly contested。主语从句内部包含三个并列成分：legal age limits / advice / a focus on what happens in classrooms。
【参考译文】法律年龄限制、建议，还是关注课堂上发生的事情，究竟哪种是最佳应对方式，这一点存在激烈争议。

Whether legal age limits, advice, or a focus on what happens in classrooms is the best response is highly contested/kənˈtestɪd/ 争论的，质疑的. While some regard Australia's ban on social media use by under-16s as the start of a global pushback/ˈpʊʃbæk/ 反击，抵制 against an over-mighty industry, others are ready to dismiss/dɪsˈmɪs/ 驳回，不予理会 the policy as a failure four months after it was introduced.

Clearly not all tech is the same. But the point being made with growing confidence/ˈkɒnfɪdəns/ 信心，把握 by researchers is that not all brains are the same either. Children and adolescents have specific needs and vulnerabilities/ˌvʌlnərəˈbɪlətiz/ 脆弱性，弱点 and are at risk of being harmed when these are targeted/ˈtɑːɡɪtɪd/ 针对，以...为目标 by businesses. Whistleblowers/ˈwɪsblˌbloʊərz/ 举报人，吹哨人 including Frances Haugen have highlighted teenagers' susceptibility/səˌseptəˈbɪləti/ 易感性，敏感性 to the preoccupations/priˌɒkjuˈpeɪʃənz/ 全神贯注，忧虑 with status and appearance that are used to maximise/ˈmæksɪmaɪz/ 最大化 engagement/ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/ 参与度，互动. Early-years experts such as Prof Sam Wass are convinced that the brain and language development of the youngest children is hindered/ˈhɪndəd/ 阻碍，妨碍 by hyper-stimulating, attention-grabbing content.

"Whistleblowers including Frances Haugen have highlighted teenagers' susceptibility to the preoccupations with status and appearance that are used to maximise engagement."
【结构分析】主语：Whistleblowers；谓语：have highlighted；宾语：teenagers' susceptibility to the preoccupations。that引导定语从句修饰preoccupations，从句中使用了被动语态are used to maximise engagement。
【参考译文】包括弗朗西斯·豪根在内的举报人指出，青少年极易受到地位和外貌焦虑的影响，而这些正是被科技公司用来最大化用户参与度的手段。

From George Eliot to Martha Nussbaum, there is a long tradition/trəˈdɪʃən/ 传统 in the humanities of belief in reading as socially beneficial, because of the curiosity/ˌkjʊəriˈɒsəti/ 好奇心 and sympathy/ˈsɪmpəθi/ 同情心，共鸣 for others that it engenders/ɪnˈdʒendəz/ 产生，引起 — and this is another source of concern about the impact of replacing books with devices and their personalised/ˈpɜːrsənəlaɪzd/ 个性化的, algorithmic/ˌælɡəˈrɪðmɪk/ 算法的 feeds.

Adults are not immune/ɪˈmjuːn/ 免疫的，不受影响的 to these changes. All people exist in a dynamic/daɪˈnæmɪk/ 动态的 relationship with the tools that we use to communicate/kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt/ 交流，沟通. The dilemma/dɪˈlemə/ 困境，两难 is that while public policy is meant to be evidence-based/ˈevɪdəns beɪst/ 基于证据的, the researchers who analyse technology's impact cannot keep up with the pace/peɪs/ 步伐，速度 of development.

"The dilemma is that while public policy is meant to be evidence-based, the researchers who analyse technology's impact cannot keep up with the pace of development."
【结构分析】主语：The dilemma；谓语：is；表语从句：that...development。表语从句内部包含while引导的让步状语从句，主句为the researchers...cannot keep up with...。who analyse technology's impact为定语从句修饰researchers。
【参考译文】困境在于，虽然公共政策应当基于证据，但研究技术影响的学者们却无法跟上技术发展的步伐。

Up to now, the approach to big tech has generally been laissez-faire/ˌleɪseɪˈfeə(r)/ 自由放任的. One would need evidence from a counterfactual/ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl/ 反事实的 reality to state with confidence that this has been a mistake. But the case for a more precautionary/prɪˈkɔːʃənəri/ 预防的，谨慎的 style of regulation is at last being taken seriously where it arguably matters most — in relation to the growing and impressionable/ɪmˈpreʃənəbl/ 易受影响的 minds of children.

📝 文章大意

【核心主题】本文是英国《卫报》的一篇社论，探讨了大型科技公司在教育领域日益受到严格审查的现象。文章以英国最新通过的校园手机禁令为切入点，指出这一政策反映了公众对科技巨头的不信任正在加深。

【论证逻辑】文章首先介绍了英国校园手机禁令的政治背景，随后列举了挪威、瑞典、英国和美国加州等地限制学生屏幕时间的趋势作为证据。作者承认并非所有技术都有害，但强调儿童和青少年的大脑具有特殊的脆弱性，容易受到科技公司"最大化用户参与度"策略的伤害。最后，文章呼吁采取更为谨慎的预防性监管方式，保护正在成长中的儿童心智。

【考研价值】本文涉及科技与教育的热门话题，语言正式，结构清晰，包含多个考研高频词汇和长难句，非常适合用于考研英语阅读训练。

✔ 阅读理解练习
1【主旨大意题】What is the main idea of this editorial?
A) Mobile phones should be completely banned in all schools across the world.
B) Big tech companies are finally facing serious scrutiny regarding their impact on children's education and development.
C) Norway and Sweden have the best education policies in the world.
D) Researchers cannot keep up with the pace of technological development.
正确答案：B
文章开篇即以英国校园手机禁令引入，核心论点是大型科技公司终于受到严格审查（"Big Tech Is Finally Under the Microscope"）。A选项过于绝对（并非主张全球禁令），C选项仅为文中举例，D选项是文章提及的一个细节而非主旨。
2【推理判断题】What can be inferred from the examples of Norway and Sweden mentioned in Paragraph 3?
A) Both countries have banned all digital devices permanently.
B) Students in these countries prefer handwriting to typing.
C) The reversal of tech-friendly policies suggests that excessive screen time may harm students' academic performance.
D) Reading scores in both countries are the lowest in Europe.
正确答案：C
文章提到两国在"recorded drops in reading scores"（阅读成绩下降）后，将推广iPad和笔记本的政策"reversed in favour of books and handwriting"。由此可推断，过多的屏幕时间可能损害学生学业表现。A选项"永久禁止"过于绝对；B选项未提及；D选项"最低"文中无依据。
3【词义猜测题】The word "laissez-faire" in the last paragraph most probably means ______.
A) a policy of letting things take their own course without interference
B) a strict regulatory approach to technology companies
C) an evidence-based method of policymaking
D) a precautionary style of regulation
正确答案：A
"laissez-faire"为法语借词，原意为"放任自由"。文章最后一段将其与"a more precautionary style of regulation"（更为谨慎的预防性监管方式）形成对比，说明过去对大型科技公司的态度是"放任不管"的。B、C、D选项均与文意相反或无关。
阅读技巧提示
  • 主旨题技巧：关注文章标题和首段，社论类文章通常在开头即点明核心观点。
  • 推理题技巧：注意文中"举例"的上下文逻辑，例子往往服务于论证前文提出的观点。
  • 词义猜测技巧：利用反义对比关系（如本文"laissez-faire"与"precautionary"的对比）推断生词含义。
  • 长难句突破：遇到嵌套从句时，先找主谓结构，再逐层分析从句类型和修饰关系。
