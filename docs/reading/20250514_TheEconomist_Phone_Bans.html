📋 文章大意

本文选自《经济学人》2025年2月刊，探讨了全球范围内学校禁止使用智能手机的政策及其效果。法国自2018年起禁止中小学生在校使用手机，荷兰和匈牙利也相继效仿，英国正在考虑类似措施。然而，最新研究表明，学校手机禁令对改善学生心理健康的效果可能远不如预期。文章引用了剑桥大学和伯明翰大学的研究，指出问题远比"禁不禁"复杂——社交媒体公司拒绝向独立研究者提供数据，使得科学界难以获得准确的结论。作者呼吁采取更加精细化的、基于证据的政策，而非简单粗暴的一刀切禁令。

Early Evidence Suggests Phone Bans at Schools May Do Little to Benefit Pupils' Mental Health

France has not allowed smartphone use in primary or secondary schools since 2018, claiming that it would help children focus, reduce their social-media use and mitigate online bullying. The Netherlands initiated a similar ban in January 2024. Hungary followed suit later that year. Legislators in Britain are considering similar measures. The key question facing them is whether banning smartphones in schools offers any benefits to mental health.

That there is a problem seems clear. In 2021 America's surgeon general compiled a report revealing that persistent feelings of hopelessness climbed by 40% among American high-school pupils between 2009 and 2019. The number who seriously considered killing themselves went up by 36%. What makes these findings all the worse is that 48% of mental-health problems (like depression and anxiety) that emerge during adolescence will plague people for the rest of their lives.

It is tempting to connect these trends with the increased availability of smartphones, but establishing a causal connection is difficult. Part of the problem is that smartphones contain multitudes. Using a smartphone to solve crosswords or read the news may well have markedly different psychological effects from intensive social-media use.

Children are no less varied. The brain undergoes profound changes during puberty, meaning that any research on the effects of smartphone use needs to consider the developmental age of the children being studied as well as their precise smartphone habits.

No studies have got this specific. Those that have come close, however, reveal that unfettered access to social media on smartphones during puberty, especially at critical moments when the brain is changing, may cause problems. One study, led by Amy Orben at the University of Cambridge, asked 17,409 people between the ages of ten and 21 how satisfied they were with their lives and how much they used social media.

The findings, reported in 2022 in Nature Communications, show that girls who increased their social media use over the course of a year were significantly less satisfied with their lives if the increase took place when they were between 11 and 13. Boys showed the same trend when increases took place when they were 14 or 15 years old.

How much of this will change by banning phones in schools is unclear. In a paper published in The Lancet in February, Victoria Goodyear at the University of Birmingham compared the mental well-being of students in schools that implemented restrictive smartphone policies with those with relaxed policies. She also monitored overall screentime. Her results show that, though those who spent more time on a smartphone overall did have a decline in mental well-being, there was no difference between the two groups. She and her colleagues argue that setting up policies at schools alone is simply not enough.

Researchers are trying to paint a complete picture with both hands tied behind their back. According to Dr Orben, social-media companies routinely refuse to give independent researchers access to detailed data on the behaviors of their users. This forces researchers to rely on less accurate proxy measures, like overall screentime. It also means that children playing educational games are being put in the same analytical bin as children who are on social media. A more nuanced picture of the effects of smartphones needs to be drawn.

🔍 长难句解析

"What makes these findings all the worse is that 48% of mental-health problems (like depression and anxiety) that emerge during adolescence will plague people for the rest of their lives."
结构分析：
• 主语从句：What makes these findings all the worse（让这些发现更糟糕的是……）
• 系动词：is
• 表语从句：that 48% of mental-health problems...will plague people...
• 定语从句：that emerge during adolescence（修饰mental-health problems）
• 时间状语：for the rest of their lives

翻译：让这些发现更加令人担忧的是，在青春期出现的心理健康问题（如抑郁和焦虑）中有48%将会困扰人们余生。
"It is tempting to connect these trends with the increased availability of smartphones, but establishing a causal connection is difficult."
结构分析：
• 形式主语句：It is tempting to connect...（人们很容易将……联系起来）
• 并列连词：but（转折）
• 动名词短语作主语：establishing a causal connection（建立因果关系）
• 系表结构：is difficult

翻译：人们很容易将这些趋势与智能手机的日益普及联系起来，但建立因果关系却很困难。
"The findings, reported in 2022 in Nature Communications, show that girls who increased their social media use over the course of a year were significantly less satisfied with their lives if the increase took place when they were between 11 and 13."
结构分析：
• 主句：The findings show that...
• 插入语：reported in 2022 in Nature Communications（发表于2022年《自然·通讯》）
• 宾语从句：that girls...were significantly less satisfied...
• 定语从句：who increased their social media use over the course of a year（修饰girls）
• 条件状语从句：if the increase took place when they were between 11 and 13
• 时间状语从句嵌套：when they were between 11 and 13

翻译：2022年发表在《自然·通讯》上的研究结果显示，如果在一年中增加了社交媒体使用量的行为发生在11到13岁之间，女孩对生活的满意度会显著降低。
"Researchers are trying to paint a complete picture with both hands tied behind their back."
结构分析：
• 主句：Researchers are trying to paint a complete picture
• 方式状语（独立结构/比喻）：with both hands tied behind their back
• 这是一个生动的比喻，字面意思是"双手被绑在背后画画"，实际含义是研究者在缺乏关键数据的情况下艰难地试图得出全面结论

翻译：研究者们试图描绘一幅完整的图景，但困难重重——他们就像双手被绑在背后一样，举步维艰。

✏️ 阅读理解练习（考研风格）

主旨大意题

1. What is the main idea of this passage?

正确答案：B
解析：文章的核心论点是学校手机禁令对改善学生心理健康的效果远不如预期。文章引用多项研究指出，虽然青少年心理健康问题确实存在，但简单禁止手机并不能解决问题。作者强调需要更精细化的、基于证据的政策（"A more nuanced picture...needs to be drawn"），而非一刀切的禁令。A项过于绝对，C项与文章"establishing a causal connection is difficult"矛盾，D项曲解了文章立场。
细节理解题

2. According to Victoria Goodyear's research, what did she find about school phone bans?

正确答案：C
解析：根据第七段，Goodyear博士比较了实施限制性手机政策的学校与政策宽松的学校的学生心理健康状况，发现"there was no difference between the two groups"（两组之间没有差异）。虽然总屏幕时间较多的学生心理健康确实有所下降，但这一差异与学校是否实施手机禁令无关。A、B、D项均与文章内容不符。
推理判断题

3. What can be inferred about the challenges researchers face in studying the effects of smartphones on mental health?

正确答案：C
解析：最后一段明确指出两大障碍：一是"social-media companies routinely refuse to give independent researchers access to detailed data"（社交媒体公司拒绝提供详细数据），二是"smartphones contain multitudes"（智能手机功能多样，使用方式因人而异），导致研究者只能依赖"less accurate proxy measures"。A项与文章矛盾，B项与"Children are no less varied"矛盾，D项与"establishing a causal connection is difficult"矛盾。

💡 考研阅读技巧

  • 关注转折与让步：文中"but"、"however"、"though"等词是作者观点的关键信号。例如"but establishing a causal connection is difficult"直接否定了前文的简单归因。
  • 识别比喻与修辞："paint a complete picture with both hands tied behind their back"是生动比喻，考研常考此类修辞手法的理解。
  • 区分相关与因果：本文核心考点之一是"correlation does not imply causation"（相关性不等于因果关系），这是考研阅读常见逻辑考点。
  • 注意数据引用：文章引用了具体数字（40%、36%、48%）和权威来源（Nature Communications、The Lancet），这些细节常作为细节题的出题依据。
  • 词汇积累重点：mitigate、plague、nuanced、unfettered、proxy等是考研高频词，需重点记忆。
  • 把握文章结构：本文采用"现象→数据→研究→结论"的结构，最后一段提出深层问题（数据不透明），这是典型的经济学人文章结构。
