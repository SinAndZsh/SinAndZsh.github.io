Students and AI: Mastery, Not Misuse

来源：The Christian Science Monitor 日期：2026年7月3日 话题：科技 / 教育 词数：约1,200词
CSM
The Christian Science Monitor (基督教科学箴言报) — 创办于1908年，是美国著名的全国性日报，以深度报道和社论闻名。该报以"不伤害任何人"为宗旨，报道客观、分析深入，其社论栏目常就教育、科技与社会发展等议题发表独到见解，是考研英语阅读的重要题源之一。
📖 正文精读

Editorial: The debate over artificial intelligence in education should focus less on banning the technology and more on teaching students how to use it responsibly and effectively.

As artificial intelligence/ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃəl ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns/ 人工智能 tools become increasingly commonplace/ˈkɒmənpleɪs/ 普遍的，司空见惯的 in classrooms and workplaces, a fierce/fɪəs/ 激烈的，猛烈的 debate has erupted/ɪˈrʌptɪd/ 爆发，突然发生 over whether these technologies are likely to improve — or doom/duːm/ 使注定失败，使毁灭 — education. School districts across the United States have scrambled/ˈskræmbld/ 争抢，仓促行动 to formulate/ˈfɔːmjuleɪt/ 制定，构想 policies that either embrace/ɪmˈbreɪs/ 欣然接受，拥抱 AI as an innovative/ˈɪnəveɪtɪv/ 创新的 teaching aid or restrict/rɪˈstrɪkt/ 限制，约束 its use amid fears of academic dishonesty/ˌækəˈdemɪs dɪˈɒnɪsti/ 学术不端. Yet beneath this polarised/ˈpɒləraɪzd/ 两极分化的 rhetoric/ˈretərɪk/ rhetoric，言论 lies a more nuanced/ˈnjuːɑːnst/ 微妙的，有细微差别的 question: How can we ensure that students master these powerful tools rather than become subservient/səbˈsɜːviənt/ 屈从的，依附的 to them?

Many educators worry that AI tools could encourage cheating or reduce critical thinking/ˈkrɪtɪkl ˈθɪŋkɪŋ/ 批判性思维 if students simply copy answers generated by chatbots. Research and policy discussions have raised concerns that over-reliance on AI may create a "false mastery/fɔːls ˈmɑːstəri/ 虚假掌握," where students produce polished/ˈpɒlɪʃt/ 完美的，打磨过的 results without fully understanding the underlying/ˌʌndəˈlaɪɪŋ/ 根本的，潜在的 concepts. This could weaken essential skills such as analysis, reasoning, and creativity if AI is treated as a replacement for learning rather than a support tool. The temptation/tempˈteɪʃn/ 诱惑 to take shortcuts/ˈʃɔːtkʌts/ 捷径 is particularly strong among students who face mounting/ˈmaʊntɪŋ/ 不断增加的 pressure to excel/ɪkˈsel/ 出类拔萃，擅长 in an increasingly competitive/kəmˈpetətɪv/ 竞争激烈的 academic environment.

"Research and policy discussions have raised concerns that over-reliance on AI may create a 'false mastery,' where students produce polished results without fully understanding the underlying concepts."
【结构分析】主语：Research and policy discussions；谓语：have raised；宾语：concerns。that引导同位语从句解释concerns的内容，其中包含where引导的非限制性定语从句修饰"false mastery"。
【参考译文】研究和政策讨论已经引发了担忧：过度依赖人工智能可能会制造一种"虚假掌握"——学生能够产出完美的结果，却没有真正理解其背后的概念。

However, the prohibition/ˌprəʊɪˈbɪʃn/ 禁止，阻止 of AI in educational settings is neither feasible/ˈfiːzəbl/ 可行的 nor desirable/dɪˈzaɪərəbl/ 可取的，理想的. Artificial intelligence will likely be an integral/ˈɪntɪɡrəl/ 不可或缺的，完整的 part of students' professional lives, and shielding/ˈʃiːldɪŋ/ 保护，屏蔽 them from these technologies during their formative years would leave them ill-equipped/ˌɪl ɪˈkwɪpt/ 装备不良的，准备不足的 for the workforce/ˈwɜːrkfɔːrs/ 劳动力，员工队伍 they will soon enter. Instead of focusing solely on restrictions, schools should teach AI literacy. Students need to understand how AI systems work, their limitations/ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃnz/ 局限性，限制, and how to verify/ˈverɪfaɪ/ 核实，验证 the accuracy/ˈækjərəsi/ 准确性 of AI-generated information.

By learning how to question/ˈkwestʃən/ 质疑，提问 AI outputs and use them as research assistants or brainstorming/ˈbreɪnstɔːmɪŋ/ 头脑风暴 tools, students can strengthen/ˈstreŋθn/ 加强，巩固 their knowledge rather than bypass/ˈbaɪpɑːs/ 绕过，回避 the learning process. This approach cultivates/ˈkʌltɪveɪts/ 培养，陶冶 what educators call "metacognitive/ˌmetəˈkɒɡnɪtɪv/ 元认知的 awareness" — the ability to think about one's own thinking. When students evaluate/ɪˈvæljueɪt/ 评估，评价 whether an AI response is plausible/ˈplɔːzəbl/ 貌似合理的, relevant/ˈreləvənt/ 相关的，切题的, and well-substantiated/səbˈstænʃieɪtɪd/ 有根据的，被证实的, they are practicing the very critical thinking skills that critics fear AI will erode/ɪˈrəʊd/ 侵蚀，逐渐削弱.

"When students evaluate whether an AI response is plausible, relevant, and well-substantiated, they are practicing the very critical thinking skills that critics fear AI will erode."
【结构分析】主句主语：they；谓语：are practicing；宾语：the very critical thinking skills。When引导时间状语从句，其中包含whether引导的宾语从句。that引导定语从句修饰skills，从句中critics为主语，fear为谓语，AI will erode为宾语从句（省略了that）。
【参考译文】当学生评估人工智能的回应是否可信、相关且有充分依据时，他们正是在练习那些批评者担心人工智能会侵蚀的批判性思维技能。

Some pioneering/ˌpaɪəˈnɪərɪŋ/ 开创性的 schools have already begun to implement/ˈɪmplɪment/ 实施，执行 this pedagogical/ˌpedəˈɡɒdʒɪkl/ 教学法的，教育学的 shift/ʃɪft/ 转变，转换. In these classrooms, AI is not a forbidden/fəˈbɪdn/ 被禁止的 crutch/krʌtʃ/ 拐杖，依赖 but a tangible/ˈtændʒəbl/ 有形的，可触摸的 subject of study. Students learn about algorithmic bias/ˌælɡəˈrɪðmɪk ˈbaɪəs/ 算法偏见, data privacy/ˈdeɪtə ˈprɪvəsi/ 数据隐私, and the ethical implications/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃnz/ 含义，影响 of automated/ˈɔːtəmeɪtɪd/ 自动化的 decision-making. They experiment with prompt engineering/ˈprɒmpt ˌendʒɪˈnɪərɪŋ/ 提示词工程 — the craft of formulating/ˈfɔːmjuleɪtɪŋ/ 构思，制定 questions to elicit better responses from AI systems — which simultaneously/ˌsɪmlˈteɪniəsli/ 同时地 teaches them precision/prɪˈsɪʒn/ 精确性 in language and clarity/ˈklærəti/ 清晰度 in thought. These students are not passive/ˈpæsɪv/ 被动的 recipients/rɪˈsɪpiənts/ 接受者 of information but active interrogators/ɪnˈterəɡeɪtəz/ 审问者，质疑者 of technology.

Ultimately, the editorial argues that education systems must adapt to technological/ˌteknəˈlɒdʒɪkl/ 技术的 change rather than resist/rɪˈzɪst/ 抵制，抗拒 it. The goal should not be to eliminate AI from education but to guide students toward responsible and informed use, ensuring that technology enhances learning instead of undermining it. Schools face the profound/prəˈfaʊnd/ 深远的，深刻的 task of preparing students for a future where AI is an everyday part of learning and work. This requires a fundamental/ˌfʌndəˈmentl/ 根本的，基础的 rethinking/ˌriːˈθɪŋkɪŋ/ 重新思考 of what it means to be educated in the twenty-first century — a reimagining/ˌriːɪˈmædʒɪnɪŋ/ 重新构想 that places human judgment/ˈdʒʌdʒmənt/ 判断力 and intellectual/ˌɪntəˈlektʃuəl/ 智力的，知识的 autonomy/ɔːˈtɒnəmi/ 自主性 at the centre of the educational enterprise/ˈentəpraɪz/ 事业，企业.

"This requires a fundamental rethinking of what it means to be educated in the twenty-first century — a reimagining that places human judgment and intellectual autonomy at the centre of the educational enterprise."
【结构分析】主语：This；谓语：requires；宾语：a fundamental rethinking。of what引导宾语从句作rethinking的定语。破折号后为同位语a reimagining，后面跟that引导的定语从句修饰reimagining。
【参考译文】这要求我们从根本上重新思考"在二十一世纪接受教育"意味着什么——一种将人类判断力和知识自主性置于教育事业的核心的重新构想。
📝 文章大意

【核心主题】本文是《基督教科学箴言报》的一篇社论，探讨了人工智能在教育领域的应用争议。文章指出，当前关于AI在教育中的辩论往往陷入两极分化——要么全面拥抱，要么彻底禁止——而真正的挑战在于如何引导学生掌握这些技术，而非被技术所奴役。

【论证逻辑】文章首先指出过度依赖AI可能导致"虚假掌握"的风险，即学生产出完美结果却不理解背后概念；随后论证完全禁止AI既不可行也不可取，因为AI将成为学生未来职业生活的重要组成部分；接着提出解决方案——学校应教授AI素养，让学生学会质疑AI输出、验证信息准确性，将AI作为研究助手而非学习捷径；最后呼吁教育体系适应技术变革，将人类判断力和知识自主性置于教育的核心。

【考研价值】本文聚焦AI与教育这一持续热门话题，语言正式、论证严密，包含大量考研高频词汇（如integral, feasible, substantiated, erode等）和结构复杂的长难句，非常适合用于考研英语阅读训练。

✔ 阅读理解练习
1【主旨大意题】What is the main argument of this editorial?
A) Artificial intelligence should be completely banned from all schools to protect students' critical thinking skills.
B) Schools should teach students to use AI responsibly rather than simply restricting its use.
C) AI tools are fundamentally harmful to education and will inevitably doom the learning process.
D) Students should be shielded from all technologies during their formative years.
正确答案：B
文章核心论点是：与其禁止AI，不如教会学生负责任地使用它。文章标题即点明主旨"Mastery, Not Misuse"，第一段也明确指出辩论应"focus less on banning the technology and more on teaching students how to use it responsibly"。A、C、D选项均与文章观点相反。
2【词义猜测题】The phrase "false mastery" in Paragraph 2 most probably means ______.
A) a genuine and deep understanding of complex concepts
B) a teaching method that relies heavily on artificial intelligence
C) an illusion of competence without true understanding
D) a strict evaluation system for AI-generated content
正确答案：C
"false mastery"字面意思为"虚假掌握"。文章紧接着解释："where students produce polished results without fully understanding the underlying concepts"（学生产出完美结果却没有真正理解背后的概念）。因此，"false mastery"指的是一种没有真正理解的、表面上的能力。C选项"an illusion of competence without true understanding"（没有真正理解的能力幻觉）最符合文意。
3【推理判断题】What can be inferred from the editorial's discussion of "prompt engineering"?
A) It is a skill that only professional programmers need to learn.
B) It will soon replace traditional writing courses in schools.
C) Learning to craft effective prompts can simultaneously develop students' language precision and clarity of thought.
D) Most schools have already abandoned this approach due to its complexity.
正确答案：C
文章第四段明确指出，prompt engineering（提示词工程）"simultaneously teaches them precision in language and clarity in thought"（同时教会他们语言的精确性和思维的清晰度）。C选项是对原文的同义改写。A选项"只有专业程序员需要"与文意不符；B选项"取代传统写作课"文中未提及；D选项"大多数学校已放弃"与原文"Some pioneering schools have already begun to implement"矛盾。
阅读技巧提示
  • 主旨题技巧：社论类文章的主旨通常在标题和首段即明确呈现，注意寻找表明作者立场的关键词（如"should focus on...rather than..."）。
  • 词义猜测技巧：遇到带引号的短语时，注意其后的同位语从句或定语从句，这些往往是出题人设置的解释线索（如本文"false mastery"后的where从句）。
  • 推理题技巧：正确选项往往是原文的同义改写而非直接引用，需警惕选项中的绝对化表述（如only, all, most等）。
  • 长难句突破：遇到嵌套从句时，先剥离修饰成分找出主干，再逐层还原从句含义。
